# Marc Andreessen - Interview with Reid Hoffman (Masters of Scale)

**Date:** 2017  
**Source:** Masters of Scale Podcast  
**Interviewer:** Reid Hoffman

---

## About This Interview

Two legendary figures in Silicon Valley discuss scaling companies, the nature of entrepreneurship, and the future of technology.

---

## The Interview

### On Scaling

**Reid Hoffman:** You've scaled multiple companies. What's the key?

**Marc Andreessen:** Scaling is about three things:

**1. Product-market fit**
You can't scale without it. You can grow, but you can't scale.

**2. Culture**
As you grow, culture becomes your operating system. It determines how decisions get made.

**3. Hiring**
At scale, you're only as good as your people. Hiring becomes your most important job.

### On Product-Market Fit

**Hoffman:** You coined the term "product-market fit." What is it?

**Andreessen:** Product-market fit is when you've built something that the market wants. Not just likes—wants desperately.

Signs of PMF:
- Usage grows organically
- Retention is strong
- Customers tell others
- You can't keep up with demand

Before PMF, everything is hard. After PMF, everything gets easier.

**Hoffman:** How do you know if you don't have it?

**Andreessen:** If you have to push for every sale, every user, every engagement—you don't have it.

If customers are pulling, if they're demanding more—you might have it.

### On Culture

**Hoffman:** How do you build culture at scale?

**Andreessen:** Culture is what happens when you're not in the room. It's the default behavior.

You build it by:
1. Being explicit about values
2. Hiring people who embody those values
3. Rewarding behavior that matches values
4. Removing people who don't fit

**Hoffman:** What happens if you get culture wrong?

**Andreessen:** It compounds. Bad culture gets worse as you scale. Politics, bureaucracy, mediocrity.

Netscape had this problem. We grew too fast. Hired too quickly. Culture got diluted.

### On Competition

**Hoffman:** How do you think about competition?

**Andreessen:** Peter Thiel says "competition is for losers." I agree.

The best companies create monopolies. Not through illegal means—through being so good that no one can compete.

Google has a monopoly on search. Not because they crushed competitors—because they're just better.

**Hoffman:** What about when competition comes?

**Andreessen:** If you're truly differentiated, competition doesn't matter. If you're not, you're in a commodity business. And that's a race to the bottom.

### On the Future

**Hoffman:** What are you most excited about?

**Andreessen:** Three areas:

**1. AI**
We're at the beginning of the AI revolution. It will transform every industry.

**2. Crypto/Web3**
A new layer of the Internet. Decentralized, programmable, owned by users.

**3. Biotech**
We're learning to read and write the code of life. The implications are staggering.

### On Failure

**Hoffman:** You've had public failures. How do you handle them?

**Andreessen:** Failure is information. It tells you what doesn't work.

Netscape lost to Microsoft. Loudcloud was too early. Both taught me lessons I use today.

The key is to fail fast and learn. Don't fail slowly. That's expensive.

### On Advice for Founders

**Hoffman:** What's your advice for founders?

**Andreessen:** Five things:

**1. Pick the right market**
A great team in a bad market will fail. A mediocre team in a great market can succeed.

**2. Move fast**
Speed is your advantage over big companies. Use it.

**3. Focus**
Do one thing really well. Don't try to do everything.

**4. Hire slowly**
One bad hire can destroy a startup. Be patient.

**5. Don't die**
Startups die when founders give up. Don't give up.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. PMF Is Everything
Before product-market fit, nothing else matters.

### 2. Culture Compounds
Good culture gets better. Bad culture gets worse.

### 3. Competition Is for Losers
Create monopolies through differentiation, not through crushing competitors.

### 4. Speed Is Advantage
Move faster than big companies can.

### 5. Don't Die
Persistence is the ultimate startup advantage.
