# Charlie Munger - Interview with Yahoo Finance (2020)

**Date:** April 2020  
**Source:** Yahoo Finance  
**Interviewer:** Andy Serwer

---

## About This Interview

During the COVID-19 pandemic, Munger discusses market volatility, government response, and his outlook for the future.

---

## The Interview

### On the Pandemic

**Andy Serwer:** How are you handling the pandemic?

**Charlie Munger:** I'm 96 years old. I'm being careful. But I'm not panicking. This too shall pass.

**Serwer:** Are you worried about the economy?

**Munger:** In the short term, yes. It's going to be painful. But in the long term, no. Human beings are resilient. We'll get through this.

### On the Market

**Serwer:** The market has been incredibly volatile. What's your take?

**Munger:** Markets are always volatile. This is just more so. If you can't handle volatility, you shouldn't be in the market.

**Serwer:** Are you buying?

**Munger:** We're always looking. But we're patient. We don't have to do anything. That's an advantage.

### On Government Response

**Serwer:** What do you think of the government stimulus?

**Munger:** I think they had to do something. Whether they did the right thing, we'll see. It's unprecedented.

**Serwer:** Are you worried about inflation?

**Munger:** Eventually, yes. You can't print this much money without consequences. But when? I don't know.

### On Oil Prices

**Serwer:** Oil went negative. Did you ever think you'd see that?

**Munger:** No. It's remarkable. But it shows you how quickly things can change. One day everyone wants oil. The next day nobody does.

### On Berkshire's Cash

**Serwer:** Berkshire has a lot of cash. Are you going to deploy it?

**Munger:** When we find the right opportunity. We're not in a hurry. Having cash when others don't is an advantage.

**Serwer:** Some people criticize you for having too much cash.

**Munger:** Let them criticize. We've done pretty well over the years being patient.

### On the Future

**Serwer:** What does the world look like after this?

**Munger:** Different. More remote work. Less business travel. More government debt. But human beings adapt. We'll figure it out.

**Serwer:** Are you optimistic?

**Munger:** I'm always optimistic in the long run. Short term, things can be messy. But over decades, things get better.
