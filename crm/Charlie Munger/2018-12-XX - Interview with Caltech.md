# Charlie Munger - Interview with Caltech (2018)

**Date:** December 2018  
**Source:** Caltech Distinguished Alumnus Award  
**Location:** Pasadena, California

---

## About This Interview

Munger returns to his alma mater to discuss his education, career, and the principles that guided his life. Caltech shaped his scientific approach to problem-solving.

---

## The Interview

### On Caltech

**Interviewer:** How did Caltech shape you?

**Charlie Munger:** Caltech taught me to think scientifically. To question assumptions. To use first principles.

Before Caltech, I was just a smart kid. After Caltech, I was someone who understood how to solve hard problems.

**Interviewer:** What was the most important thing you learned?

**Munger:** That you can figure things out. You don't have to accept conventional wisdom. You can use physics, chemistry, mathematics to understand the world.

### On Mathematics

**Interviewer:** You're known for using mathematics in investing.

**Munger:** Yes. Compound interest is the most important concept. Probability. Statistics. These are essential.

But most people in business don't understand them. They make decisions based on gut feeling. That's a mistake.

**Interviewer:** Can you give an example?

**Munger:** Expected value. If you have a 10% chance of making $100 million, that's worth $10 million. Most people don't think that way.

### On Physics

**Interviewer:** You often reference physics.

**Munger:** Physics teaches you about complex systems. Feedback loops. Critical mass. These apply to business.

A business can reach a tipping point where it becomes self-sustaining. Like a nuclear reaction. That's what you want.

### On His Career

**Interviewer:** You started as a lawyer. How did you become an investor?

**Munger:** I realized I could make more money investing than practicing law. And I enjoyed it more.

But my legal training was useful. It taught me to read carefully. To spot flaws in reasoning. To think from multiple perspectives.

**Interviewer:** And then you met Warren Buffett.

**Munger:** Yes. We were both from Omaha. We had similar values. We both loved learning. It was a natural partnership.

### On Reading

**Interviewer:** You read constantly. Why?

**Munger:** To learn. To understand. To make better decisions.

I read history, science, biography, psychology. Everything connects. The more you know, the better your mental models.

**Interviewer:** What are you reading now?

**Munger:** Everything. I read the Wall Street Journal, the Financial Times, scientific journals. I read books on history. I read biographies.

I don't have a system. I just read what interests me.

### On Ethics

**Interviewer:** You've emphasized ethics in business.

**Munger:** Of course. If you don't have integrity, you have nothing. People won't trust you. You won't sleep well.

Warren and I have always tried to do the right thing. Not because we're saints, but because it works. Trust is the most valuable asset.

### On Longevity

**Interviewer:** You're 95. What's your secret?

**Munger:** I don't have a secret. Genetics, probably. I don't drink. I don't smoke. I try to stay mentally active.

But mostly luck. I've been lucky in so many ways.

### On Advice for Students

**Interviewer:** What advice would you give Caltech students?

**Munger:** Three things:

**1. Keep learning**
Never stop. The world changes. You have to change with it.

**2. Be rational**
Emotions are the enemy of good decisions. Use your head.

**3. Have integrity**
Do the right thing. Always. It's the only way to live.
