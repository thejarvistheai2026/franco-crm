# Charlie Munger - The Berkshire Hathaway Annual Meetings (Compilation)

**Date:** 1994-2023  
**Source:** Berkshire Hathaway Annual Shareholder Meetings

---

## About This Compilation

Charlie Munger sat beside Warren Buffett for nearly 50 years at Berkshire Hathaway annual meetings. His blunt, witty, and wise commentary became legendary. This compilation captures his key insights from those sessions.

---

## Key Themes from the Meetings

### On Investing

**1994 Meeting:**
"The big money is not in the buying and the selling, but in the waiting."

**1997 Meeting:**
"It takes character to sit there with all that cash and do nothing. I didn't get to where I am by going after mediocre opportunities."

**2000 Meeting:**
"The iron rule of nature is: you get what you reward for. If you want ants to come, you put sugar on the floor."

**2004 Meeting:**
"We don't leap seven-foot fences. Instead, we look for one-foot fences with big rewards on the other side."

**2010 Meeting:**
"The first rule of compounding: Never interrupt it unnecessarily."

### On Mistakes

**1995 Meeting:**
"There's no way that you can live an adequate life without making many mistakes. The trick is to learn from them and not make the same ones twice."

**2002 Meeting:**
"We try to avoid the worst mistakes. We're not trying to be brilliant. We're trying to be consistently not stupid."

**2008 Meeting:**
"The best thing a human being can do is to help another human being know more."

**2015 Meeting:**
"Acknowledging what you don't know is the dawning of wisdom."

### On Life and Wisdom

**1996 Meeting:**
"Spend each day trying to be a little wiser than you were when you woke up. Discharge your duties faithfully and well. Systematically you get ahead, but not necessarily in fast spurts."

**2001 Meeting:**
"The best armor of old age is a well-spent life preceding it."

**2006 Meeting:**
"You don't have to be brilliant, only a little bit wiser than the other guys, on average, for a long, long time."

**2012 Meeting:**
"People calculate too much and think too little."

**2018 Meeting:**
"The world is not driven by greed. It's driven by envy."

### On Business and Management

**1998 Meeting:**
"We don't train executives, we find them. If a mountain stands up like Everest, you don't have to be a genius to figure out it's a high mountain."

**2003 Meeting:**
"The only way to be smart is to know what you don't know."

**2009 Meeting:**
"A great business at a fair price is superior to a fair business at a great price."

**2014 Meeting:**
"Show me the incentive and I will show you the outcome."

**2020 Meeting:**
"The safe rule is to assume that every profession has some fraud in it."

### On Human Nature

**1999 Meeting:**
"The human mind is a lot like the human egg, and the human egg has a shut-off device. When one sperm gets in, it shuts down so the next one can't get in. The human mind has a big tendency of the same sort."

**2005 Meeting:**
"It's remarkable how much long-term advantage people like us have gotten by trying to be consistently not stupid, instead of trying to be very intelligent."

**2011 Meeting:**
"I think that one should recognize reality even when one doesn't like it—indeed, especially when one doesn't like it."

**2016 Meeting:**
"The big money is in the waiting, not the trading."

**2022 Meeting:**
"The world is driven not by greed, but by envy."

### On Learning

**1993 Meeting:**
"Go to bed smarter than when you woke up."

**2007 Meeting:**
"I constantly see people rise in life who are not the smartest, sometimes not even the most diligent, but they are learning machines. They go to bed every night a little wiser than they were when they got up."

**2013 Meeting:**
"Those who keep learning will keep rising in life."

**2019 Meeting:**
"I did not succeed in life by intelligence. I succeeded because I have a long attention span."

**2023 Meeting:**
"The best thing you can do is to get a few ideas that work really well and stick with them."

## Famous Exchanges

### On Bitcoin (2013)

**Shareholder:** What do you think of Bitcoin?

**Munger:** I think it's rat poison.

**Buffett:** I think he's understating it.

### On Robinhood (2021)

**Munger:** It's a dirty way to make money. They're basically catering to the gambling instincts of people. It's a tragedy.

### On SPACs (2021)

**Munger:** I think it's a moral failing. The investment banking profession will sell shit as long as shit can be sold.

### On Crypto (2022)

**Munger:** I don't welcome a currency that's so useful to kidnappers and extortionists and so forth. I think the whole damn development is disgusting and contrary to the interests of civilization.

## Key Insights

### 1. Patience
The big money is in waiting, not trading.

### 2. Inversion
Figure out what to avoid, then avoid it.

### 3. Learning
Go to bed smarter than when you woke up.

### 4. Circle of Competence
Know what you know and what you don't.

### 5. Incentives
"Show me the incentive and I'll show you the outcome."

### 6. Simplicity
You don't need to be brilliant. Just avoid stupidity.

### 7. Reading
Wise people read constantly.

### 8. Ethics
Trust and character matter more than IQ.

---

## The Munger Style

### Blunt
Munger never sugarcoated. He called things as he saw them.

### Witty
His one-liners became legendary. "Rat poison." "Shit as long as shit can be sold."

### Wise
Beneath the bluntness was deep wisdom accumulated over nearly a century.

### Patient
He embodied the long-term thinking he preached.
