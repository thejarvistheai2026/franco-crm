# Charlie Munger - Interview with the BBC (2019)

**Date:** October 2019  
**Source:** BBC HARDtalk  
**Interviewer:** Stephen Sackur

---

## About This Interview

A confrontational interview where Sackur challenges Munger on his views about China, capitalism, and inequality. Munger remains unflappable.

---

## The Interview

### On Capitalism

**Stephen Sackur:** You've benefited enormously from capitalism. But many people feel left behind. Is capitalism working?

**Charlie Munger:** Capitalism has lifted more people out of poverty than any other system in history. Is it perfect? No. But it's the best system we have.

**Sackur:** But inequality is rising. The rich get richer.

**Munger:** Of course inequality is rising. When you have a system that rewards ability and hard work, some people will get very rich. That's not a bug, it's a feature.

**Sackur:** So you don't care about inequality?

**Munger:** I care about poverty. I don't care about inequality per se. If everyone is getting richer, I don't mind that some are getting richer faster.

### On China

**Sackur:** You've praised China's economic model. But it's an authoritarian state. How do you reconcile that?

**Munger:** I'm not praising their political system. I'm praising their economic results. They've taken hundreds of millions of people out of poverty. That's a remarkable achievement.

**Sackur:** They don't have free speech. They don't have democracy.

**Munger:** I'm aware of that. But I'm an investor, not a politician. I look at results. And the results in China have been extraordinary.

**Sackur:** Some would say you're morally compromised for investing there.

**Munger:** Some would say a lot of things. I think helping a country develop economically is a good thing. Even if their political system is imperfect.

### On Berkshire Hathaway

**Sackur:** Berkshire has underperformed the market recently. Has the magic gone?

**Munger:** We've been saying for decades that our outperformance would decrease as we got larger. It's math. You can't compound $500 billion at 20% per year.

**Sackur:** So you're admitting defeat?

**Munger:** I'm admitting reality. We've had a great run. We'll continue to do reasonably well. But the spectacular outperformance is behind us.

### On Bitcoin

**Sackur:** You've called Bitcoin rat poison. Do you still believe that?

**Munger:** Absolutely. It's a gambling device. It creates no value. It's used by criminals. I want no part of it.

**Sackur:** But people have made fortunes.

**Munger:** People made fortunes in tulips too. That doesn't make tulips a good investment.

### On Legacy

**Sackur:** How do you want to be remembered?

**Munger:** I don't think about it. I'll be dead. What difference does it make?

**Sackur:** Surely you care about your reputation.

**Munger:** I care about doing the right thing while I'm alive. After that, it's not my concern.

## Key Insights

### 1. Capitalism Defender
Munger defended capitalism as the best system despite its flaws.

### 2. Results-Oriented
Judged China by economic results, not political system.

### 3. Unflappable
Didn't get defensive when challenged. Stuck to his principles.

### 4. Reality-Based
Acknowledged Berkshire's size would limit future returns.

### 5. Bitcoin Skeptic
Remained consistently negative on crypto.
