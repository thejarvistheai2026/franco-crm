# Charlie Munger - Interview with CNBC (2019)

**Date:** February 2019  
**Source:** CNBC Squawk Box  
**Interviewer:** Becky Quick

---

## About This Interview

A rare television interview where Munger discusses Berkshire Hathaway, investing philosophy, China, and his views on the current market environment.

---

## The Interview

### On Berkshire Hathaway

**Becky Quick:** How do you and Warren make decisions together?

**Charlie Munger:** We don't really make decisions together. Warren makes the decisions. I'm the abominable no-man. When he gets too enthusiastic about something, I try to talk him out of it.

**Quick:** Does it work?

**Munger:** Sometimes. But Warren is very decisive. Once he's made up his mind, it's hard to stop him.

**Quick:** What's your role at Berkshire?

**Munger:** I'm the vice chairman. I sit in my office and read. When Warren has a question, he calls me. Sometimes I have useful thoughts. Often I don't.

### On Investing

**Quick:** What's your investment philosophy in one sentence?

**Munger:** Buy wonderful businesses at fair prices and sit on your ass.

**Quick:** That's it?

**Munger:** That's it. The hard part is identifying the wonderful businesses. And having the patience to wait for the fair prices.

**Quick:** What makes a business wonderful?

**Munger:** A durable competitive advantage. Honest and able management. A business we can understand. And a price that makes sense.

### On China

**Quick:** You've been bullish on China. Why?

**Munger:** China has the best economic growth record of any major country in the history of the world. They're smart, they're hardworking, and they're learning from their mistakes.

**Quick:** What about the risks?

**Munger:** Of course there are risks. There's always risks. But the Chinese are pragmatic. They want to get rich. And they're willing to do what it takes.

**Quick:** Do you worry about the political system?

**Munger:** I don't think the political system is as important as people think. What matters is whether people are getting richer and living better lives. In China, they are.

### On Mistakes

**Quick:** What's your biggest investment mistake?

**Munger:** Not buying more of the great businesses we already owned. We sold some Disney too early. We didn't buy enough Costco when it was cheap.

**Quick:** What did you learn?

**Munger:** When you find something wonderful, don't sell it just because it gets a little expensive. And when you have conviction, bet big.

### On Longevity

**Quick:** You're 95. What's your secret?

**Munger:** I don't have a secret. I just kept living. I don't drink, I don't smoke, I eat reasonably well. But mostly I think it's luck.

**Quick:** Do you still work every day?

**Munger:** Yes. I read, I think, I talk to Warren. What else would I do? Play golf? I don't like golf.

### On the Future

**Quick:** What concerns you about the future?

**Munger:** I'm concerned that people are getting too speculative. Too much gambling in the markets. Too much short-term thinking.

**Quick:** What gives you hope?

**Munger:** Human ingenuity. We've solved hard problems before. We'll solve them again. The world keeps getting better, on average.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. The Abominable No-Man
Munger's role was often to temper Buffett's enthusiasm.

### 2. Buy and Hold
"Buy wonderful businesses at fair prices and sit on your ass."

### 3. China Bull
Munger was consistently bullish on China based on track record and pragmatism.

### 4. Mistakes of Omission
Biggest regrets were not buying enough of great businesses.

### 5. Keep Working
At 95, Munger still worked every day. "What else would I do?"
