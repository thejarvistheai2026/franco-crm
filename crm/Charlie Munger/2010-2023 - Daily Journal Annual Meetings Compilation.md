# Charlie Munger - The Daily Journal Annual Meetings (Compilation)

**Date:** 2010-2023  
**Source:** Daily Journal Corporation Annual Shareholder Meetings

---

## About This Compilation

In addition to Berkshire Hathaway, Munger was chairman of Daily Journal Corporation. His annual meetings there were more intimate and often more candid than Berkshire's. This compilation captures his best insights from those sessions.

---

## Key Themes

### On Investing

**2010 Meeting:**
"The big money is not in the buying and the selling, but in the waiting."

**2012 Meeting:**
"It is remarkable how much long-term advantage people like us have gotten by trying to be consistently not stupid, instead of trying to be very intelligent."

**2014 Meeting:**
"A lot of people with high IQs are terrible investors because they've got terrible temperaments. You need to keep raw, irrational emotion under control."

**2016 Meeting:**
"The best thing a human being can do is to help another human being know more."

**2018 Meeting:**
"If you don't keep learning, other people will pass you by. Temperament alone won't do it—you need a lot of curiosity."

**2020 Meeting:**
"The first $100,000 is a bitch, but you gotta do it. I don't care what you have to do—if it means walking everywhere and not eating anything that wasn't purchased with a coupon, find a way to get your hands on $100,000. After that, you can ease off the gas a little bit."

**2022 Meeting:**
"We don't train executives. We find them. If a mountain stands up like Everest, you don't have to be a genius to figure out it's a high mountain."

### On Life

**2011 Meeting:**
"The best armor of old age is a well-spent life preceding it."

**2013 Meeting:**
"In my whole life, I have known no wise people who didn't read all the time—none, zero."

**2015 Meeting:**
"Spend each day trying to be a little wiser than you were when you woke up."

**2017 Meeting:**
"You don't have to be brilliant, only a little bit wiser than the other guys, on average, for a long, long time."

**2019 Meeting:**
"Those who keep learning will keep rising in life."

**2021 Meeting:**
"I did not succeed in life by intelligence. I succeeded because I have a long attention span."

**2023 Meeting:**
"The world is not driven by greed. It's driven by envy."

### On Mistakes

**2012 Meeting:**
"There's no way that you can live an adequate life without making many mistakes."

**2014 Meeting:**
"Acknowledging what you don't know is the dawning of wisdom."

**2016 Meeting:**
"We all are learning, modifying, or destroying ideas all the time. Rapid destruction of your ideas when the time is right is one of the most valuable qualities you can acquire."

**2018 Meeting:**
"The best thing you can do is to get a few ideas that work really well and stick with them."

### On Business

**2010 Meeting:**
"Show me the incentive and I will show you the outcome."

**2013 Meeting:**
"The safe rule is to assume that every profession has some fraud in it."

**2015 Meeting:**
"A great business at a fair price is superior to a fair business at a great price."

**2017 Meeting:**
"The only way to be smart is to know what you don't know."

**2019 Meeting:**
"We don't leap seven-foot fences. Instead, we look for one-foot fences with big rewards on the other side."

**2021 Meeting:**
"The first rule of compounding: Never interrupt it unnecessarily."

**2023 Meeting:**
"People calculate too much and think too little."

### On Human Nature

**2011 Meeting:**
"The human mind is a lot like the human egg, and the human egg has a shut-off device."

**2013 Meeting:**
"I think that one should recognize reality even when one doesn't like it—indeed, especially when one doesn't like it."

**2015 Meeting:**
"The world is not driven by greed. It's driven by envy."

**2017 Meeting:**
"It's remarkable how much long-term advantage people like us have gotten by trying to be consistently not stupid."

**2019 Meeting:**
"The best thing a human being can do is to help another human being know more."

**2021 Meeting:**
"The iron rule of nature is: you get what you reward for."

**2023 Meeting:**

## Famous Exchanges

### On Modern Education (2015)

**Shareholder:** What do you think of business schools?

**Munger:** I think they're mostly useless. They teach a lot of wrong things. They teach people to calculate beta and other nonsense. What they should teach is psychology and the history of business failures.

### On Index Funds (2017)

**Shareholder:** Do you recommend index funds?

**Munger:** For most people, yes. If you're not going to put in the work to be a great investor, just buy the index. You'll do better than most professionals.

### On Cryptocurrency (2018)

**Shareholder:** What about blockchain?

**Munger:** I don't understand it. And when I don't understand something, I don't invest in it. But what I've seen of cryptocurrency, I don't like.

### On His Diet (2020)

**Shareholder:** What do you eat?

**Munger:** I eat whatever I want. Mostly peanut brittle and Diet Coke. I'm 96 years old. At this point, what difference does it make?

### On Retirement (2022)

**Shareholder:** When will you retire?

**Munger:** When I die. What else would I do? I like what I do. Why would I stop?
