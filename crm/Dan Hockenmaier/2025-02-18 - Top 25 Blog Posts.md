## Top 25 Blog Posts Compilation

### 1. Why Growth Models Fail (2024)
**Published:** February 14, 2024  
**Source:** danhock.co  
**One-liner:** The dirty secret that growth models usually don't get adopted — and five pitfalls to avoid.  
**Key Insights:**
- Growth models are for relative tradeoff decisions, not absolute forecasts
- Separate growth models from finance operating models
- Must be in spreadsheet form to force rigor — can't hide in prose
- For complex businesses (like marketplaces), model components separately
- Requires business judgment + molecular understanding of data
- Best owned by unbiased observers (often analytics/strategic finance)
- Pitch one recommendation, not the model itself

### 2. The Concept That Explains Everything About Marketplaces (2023)
**Published:** June 27, 2023  
**Source:** danhock.co  
**One-liner:** Transaction costs (Coase/Dahlman) as the unifying theory of marketplace success and evolution.  
**Key Insights:**
- Four categories: search/info, bargaining/decision, enforcement/policing, distribution
- Marketplaces sell reduction of transaction costs, not goods/services
- Success requires high transaction costs × ability to reduce them
- TAM expansion = degree of cost elimination (Uber vs Zillow example)
- Four stages: Lead Gen → Checkout → Managed → Heavily Managed
- Each stage commands higher commission for more value created
- Demand-constrained by nature → must give value to buyers to aggregate demand
- Variable cost structure determines margin evolution

### 3. The Next Trillion Dollar Marketplace Will Put SKUs on Services (2023)
**Published:** July 27, 2023  
**Source:** danhock.co  
**One-liner:** Services marketplaces are stuck at Lead Gen because they lack "legibility" — AI will finally enable SKUs on services.  
**Key Insights:**
- Services = $1.3T freelance + $600B home improvement (US only)
- Zero public marketplaces >$10B in services (vs ~10 in goods/properties)
- "Legibility" (SKU-like info) is the barrier between Lead Gen and Checkout
- Problem: services are bespoke; customers don't know what they want
- Solution: AI chat that scopes projects in back-and-forth dialogue
- Path: AI scoping → real-time quotes → "hire now" button → managed marketplace
- Must become default search (not meta-search via Google) to win
- Low trust/low complexity services will tip first

### 4. How to Be Strategic (2024)
**Published:** September 30, 2024  
**Source:** danhock.co  
**One-liner:** The actual nature of strategic work and how to become great at it.  
**Key Insights:**
- Strategy = choosing what game to play + how to win
- Not about planning; about making hard tradeoffs with incomplete information
- Great strategists pattern-match from analogies across industries
- Build "mental model library" through diverse reading
- Strategic work is iterative: hypothesis → test → refine
- Best strategists are intellectually honest about what they don't know

### 5. Build, Sell, Understand (2025)
**Published:** July 14, 2025  
**Source:** danhock.co  
**One-liner:** A framework for navigating your startup career across three archetypal roles.  
**Key Insights:**
- Three career tracks: Build (product/engineering), Sell (growth/sales), Understand (strategy/analytics)
- Most people naturally gravitate toward one
- Best career moves expand your surface area across adjacent tracks
- "Understand" is undervalued but critical for strategic decisions
- Compensation follows scarcity: rare combinations command premiums
- Startups need all three; career grows by stacking skills

### 6. How to Choose a Startup (2023)
**Published:** December 4, 2023  
**Source:** danhock.co  
**One-liner:** Retention is by far the most important metric for assessing startup potential.  
**Key Insights:**
- Retention = best indicator of product-market fit AND driver of all other metrics
- Look at both user retention (activity) and revenue retention (expansion)
- Join when growth inflection is coming, not after it peaked
- Assess: team quality, market size, retention trends, personal growth potential
- Equity value = retention × growth × margins × TAM
- Earlier stage = higher risk, higher potential return

### 7. The Leaky Bucket Theory of Network Effects (2024)
**Published:** December 9, 2024  
**Source:** danhock.co  
**One-liner:** A framework for understanding the strength of a marketplace's network effect.  
**Key Insights:**
- Network effects are leaky buckets, not permanent moats
- Strength = how fast demand aggregates vs. how fast supply churns
- Local network effects > global (DoorDash wins by market density)
- Match rate and retention are the metrics that matter
- Network effects weaken if supply can be aggregated elsewhere
- Multi-tenanting (suppliers on multiple platforms) is the enemy

### 8. Why Did DoorDash Win? (2025)
**Published:** January 22, 2025  
**Source:** danhock.co  
**One-liner:** Three stories, all of which are true — a multi-framework analysis of marketplace dominance.  
**Key Insights:**
- Story 1: Better operational execution (speed, selection, reliability)
- Story 2: Market density and local network effects in suburbs
- Story 3: Capital deployment strategy and willingness to lose money
- All three are true simultaneously — no single explanation suffices
- Winner determined by who won in most markets, not all markets
- Execution moat erodes; density and capital advantages persist

### 9. How to Design an Org for Founder Mode (2025)
**Published:** February 7, 2025  
**Source:** danhock.co  
**One-liner:** Examples from Apple, Airbnb, Shopify, and Nvidia on maintaining founder-level involvement at scale.  
**Key Insights:**
- "Founder mode" = staying close to product and customers despite scale
- Functional orgs (Apple) enable coordination; divisional orgs enable accountability
- Best structure depends on what the company is optimizing for
- Airbnb: Brian Chesky preserved product role while delegating operations
- Shopify: Tobi Lütke stays technical, uses "trust battery" framework
- Nvidia: Jensen Huang's direct report structure and email habits

### 10. The Racecar Growth Framework (Reforge)
**Published:** 2019-2020  
**Source:** Reforge Blog  
**One-liner:** Building growth models that actually get used — the Thumbtack playbook for metric-driven growth.  
**Key Insights:**
- Growth = acquisition × activation × retention × monetization × virality
- Each team should be goaled on granular metrics that drive the primary metric
- Example: SEO team → pages indexed, content uniqueness → drives customer projects
- Models must connect to financial outcomes to get adoption
- Granular metrics enable accountability; composite metrics enable alignment

### 11. Drive Growth by Picking the Right Lane (First Round)
**Co-authored with:** Lenny Rachitsky  
**Source:** First Round Review  
**One-liner:** A customer acquisition playbook for consumer startups — choosing and optimizing growth channels.  
**Key Insights:**
- Four growth "lanes": viral, paid, organic/SEO, sales
- Best startups excel at 1-2 lanes, not all four
- Lane choice depends on product characteristics and economics
- Viral: high frequency, high social intent
- Paid: high LTV, simple funnel
- SEO: searchable intent, content scalability
- Sales: high ACV, complex sales cycles

### 12. Playing Game One (2024)
**Published:** October 8, 2024  
**Source:** danhock.co  
**One-liner:** A two-minute career manifesto for ambitious people.  
**Key Insights:**
- Career is a series of "games" — finite vs infinite
- "Game One" early career: build skills, credibility, network
- Don't optimize for pay; optimize for growth trajectory
- Best moves often look like step back on title/pay for better experience
- Compound returns from early investments dominate later outcomes

### 13. When to Join a Startup (2024)
**Published:** August 1, 2024  
**Source:** danhock.co  
**One-liner:** Learning to assess risk will help you maximize the economic outcome of joining a startup.  
**Key Insights:**
- Risk-adjusted expected value framework
- Series A/B = highest expected value for most people
- Later stage = lower risk, lower upside, more cash comp
- Earlier stage = higher variance, bigger potential outcomes
- Consider: cash needs, risk tolerance, skill development, network effects
- Join breakout companies (post-PMF, pre-scale) for best outcomes

### 14. LLMs vs. Marketplaces (2026)
**Published:** January 13, 2026  
**Source:** danhock.co  
**One-liner:** Which marketplaces will win the battle with LLMs, which will lose, and what any marketplace can do to improve its odds.  
**Key Insights:**
- LLMs threaten marketplaces by replacing search/discovery
- At-risk: Lead Gen marketplaces (low switching costs)
- Safer: Managed/Heavily Managed (operational complexity harder to replicate)
- Best defense: integrate LLMs first, own the vertical
- Data moat from transactions matters more than discovery data
- Vertical integration (owning supply) becomes more valuable

- Build "taste stack" — deep expertise across multiple domains

### 16. Vibe Analysis (2025)

### 17. How to Make Your Writing C.R.I.S.P. (2025)
**Published:** September 4, 2025  
**Source:** danhock.co  
**One-liner:** A framework for improving your business writing with templates and an LLM prompt.  
**Key Insights:**
- C.R.I.S.P. = Clear, Rigorous, Insightful, Structured, Punchy
- Clear: eliminate jargon, use active voice
- Rigorous: support claims with data, acknowledge limitations
- Insightful: answer "so what?" for every point
- Structured: logical flow, signposting, strong transitions
- Punchy: strong openers and closers, memorable phrases

**Published:** June 11, 2025

**One-liner:** AI will break fundamental trade-offs in product design and bring back massive general-purpose products.  
**Key Insights:**

### 21. Growth Loops vs. Funnels (Reforge)
**Co-authored with:** Brian Balfour, Casey Winters, Kevin Kwok, Andrew Chen  
**Source:** Reforge Blog  
**One-liner:** Why growth loops (compounding systems) outperform funnels (linear processes) for sustainable growth.  
**Key Insights:**
- Funnels = linear, require constant input, don't compound
- Loops = self-reinforcing, outputs become inputs, compound over time
- 20+ growth loop archetypes across acquisition, retention, monetization
- Examples: viral loops, content loops, paid loops, engagement loops
- Audit current growth system: what percentage comes from loops vs funnels?
- Funnels scale linearly with spend; loops scale exponentially with investment
- Best growth strategies combine multiple reinforcing loops

### 22. Monetization and Pricing Strategy (Reforge)
**Co-created track at Reforge**  
**Source:** Reforge Program Materials  
**One-liner:** Frameworks for pricing, packaging, and monetization optimization.  
**Key Insights:**
- Pricing is the highest-leverage growth lever (revenue = users × price)
- Common mistakes: underpricing, copying competitors, set-and-forget
- Value-based pricing > cost-plus pricing
- Packaging: good/better/best structures, feature differentiation
- Monetization loops: paywalls, upgrade paths, expansion revenue
- Price elasticity testing: natural experiments, survey methods, A/B tests

### 23. Product-Market Fit Isn't Enough (Brian Balfour Essay — co-cited)
**Referenced heavily in Dan's work**  
**Source:** brianbalfour.com  
**One-liner:** Four fits needed: product-market, product-channel, channel-model, model-market.  
**Key Insights:**
- PMF alone doesn't guarantee growth
- Product-channel fit: product must work with acquisition channel
- Channel-model fit: channel economics must work with business model
- Model-market fit: market size must support business model
- All four must align for sustainable growth
- Common failure: PMF with wrong channel or broken unit economics

### 24. The Adjacent User Theory (2020)
**Source:** Reforge / Lenny's Newsletter  
**One-liner:** Growth by understanding and converting users at the edge of your current market.  
**Key Insights:**
- Adjacent users = people who know about product but haven't adopted
- They represent largest growth opportunity for mature products
- Three types: awareness (don't know value), activation (tried but churned), habit (not stickiness)
- Research adjacent users to understand barriers
- Build bridges from existing users to adjacent segments
- Iterate on positioning, onboarding, and features for each cohort
