# Dan Hockenmaier - Marketplace Metrics That Matter

**Date:** 2020  
**Source:** Reforge Essay

---

## About This Essay

A deep dive into the specific metrics marketplace operators should track at each stage of growth.

---

## The Essay

### The Metrics Framework

**Dan Hockenmaier:** Marketplaces should track different metrics at different stages. Early-stage metrics won't work at scale, and vice versa.

**Stage 1: Pre-Product-Market Fit**
Focus: Prove the concept works

**Key Metrics:**
- **Manual match rate** — Can you manually match supply and demand?
- **Supplier willingness** — Will suppliers join your platform?
- **Buyer willingness** — Will buyers use your platform?
- **Transaction frequency** — How often do transactions happen?

**Stage 2: Product-Market Fit**
Focus: Find repeatable, scalable unit economics

**Key Metrics:**
- **Unit economics** — CAC < LTV?
- **Retention curves** — Do users come back?
- **Organic growth** — Are users telling others?
- **Liquidity** — Do transactions happen without manual intervention?

**Stage 3: Scale**
Focus: Optimize and expand

**Key Metrics:**
- **GMV growth** — Total transaction volume
- **Take rate optimization** — Revenue per transaction
- **Network effects strength** — Is CAC declining?
- **Retention cohorts** — Long-term user value

### Liquidity Metrics

**Hockenmaier:** Once you have basics, track liquidity obsessively:

**For service marketplaces:**
- Time to first quote
- Quote quality (do customers hire?)
- Match rate (% of requests that get quotes)

**For product marketplaces:**
- Fill rate (% of searches with relevant results)
- Time to ship
- Out-of-stock rate

**For rental/sharing marketplaces:**
- Utilization rate
- Time to booking confirmation
- Cancellation rate

### Cohort Analysis

**Hockenmaier:** Cohorts are essential for marketplaces:

**Track by acquisition month:**
- Month 1: Activation rate
- Month 3: Early retention
- Month 12: Long-term retention
- Month 24: Power user development

**Red flags:**
- Activation declining over time
- Early retention dropping
- No power user cohort emerging

### The Magic Number

**Hockenmaier:** Every marketplace has a "magic number"—the transaction frequency that leads to retention.

At Thumbtack, we found that customers who hired someone within 30 days were much more likely to return.

Find your magic number. Then optimize for it.
