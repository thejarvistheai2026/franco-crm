# Marc Andreessen - The Pmarca Blog Archive

**Date:** 2007-2009  
**Source:** Pmarca.com (Andreessen's personal blog)

---

## About This Archive

Between 2007 and 2009, Marc Andreessen wrote extensively on his personal blog "Pmarca" about startups, venture capital, technology, and business strategy. These essays became foundational reading for a generation of entrepreneurs.

---

## Key Essays

### 1. The Only Thing That Matters (June 2007)
**One-liner:** In a startup, the only thing that matters is getting to product-market fit.
**Key insight:** Before PMF, nothing else matters—not team, not business model, not funding. After PMF, everything becomes easier.
**Impact:** Coined the term "product-market fit" as we use it today.

### 2. Why Not to Do a Startup (June 2007)
**One-liner:** Startups are brutally hard and most fail. Don't do one unless you have to.
**Key insight:** The only valid reason to start a company is that you can't not do it. Everything else—money, fame, flexibility—is a bad reason.

### 3. How to Hire the Best People (July 2007)
**One-liner:** Hiring is the most important thing you do. Do it obsessively.
**Key insight:** Spend 25% of your time hiring. Never compromise. One bad hire can destroy a startup.

### 4. The Moby Dick Theory of Big Companies (August 2007)
**One-liner:** Big companies are like whales—slow, predictable, and easily avoided.
**Key insight:** Startups worry too much about competition from big companies. Big companies can't move fast enough to compete with startups.

### 5. Retaining Great People (September 2007)
**One-liner:** Once you hire great people, you have to keep them.
**Key insight:** Great people have options. You need to give them interesting work, autonomy, and a stake in the outcome.

### 6. The Psychology of Entrepreneurial Misjudgment (October 2007)
**One-liner:** Entrepreneurs are prone to specific cognitive biases.
**Key insight:** Overconfidence, confirmation bias, and sunk cost fallacy are particularly dangerous for founders.

### 7. How to Create a Truly Great Company (November 2007)
**One-liner:** Great companies are built on three things: product, people, and culture.
**Key insight:** Product comes first. Then hire great people. Culture emerges from those two.

### 8. When the VCs Say "No" (December 2007)
**One-liner:** Rejection is the default. Learn from it.
**Key insight:** Most VCs will say no. That doesn't mean your idea is bad. It might mean wrong timing, wrong investor, or you need to prove more.

### 9. The Truth About VCs (January 2008)
**One-liner:** VCs are not your friends. They're investors.
**Key insight:** VCs have their own incentives—fund size, timeline, portfolio construction. Understand them before taking money.

### 10. How to Raise Money (February 2008)
**One-liner:** Raising money is a sales process. Treat it like one.
**Key insight:** Create competition among investors. Have a timeline. Know your numbers. Close fast.

### 11. The Guide to Personal Productivity (March 2008)
**One-liner:** Don't keep a schedule. Don't answer the phone.
**Key insight:** Protect your time ruthlessly. Batch communication. Work in long blocks.

### 12. Why I Love Twitter (April 2008)
**One-liner:** Twitter is the pulse of the Internet.
**Key insight:** Real-time information flow changes everything. Twitter is the new RSS, the new chat, the new status update.

### 13. The Case for Optimism (May 2008)
**One-liner:** Things are getting better, not worse.
**Key insight:** Despite the financial crisis, technology is advancing. Innovation continues. The future is bright.

### 14. How to Be a Genius (June 2008)
**One-liner:** Genius is 1% inspiration, 99% perspiration—and the right environment.
**Key insight:** Genius requires: deep domain knowledge, time to think, freedom to explore, and a high bar for quality.

### 15. The Future of the News Business (July 2008)
**One-liner:** Newspapers are dying. News is not.
**Key insight:** The business model is broken, not the need for information. New models will emerge.

### 16. How to Think About Competition (August 2008)
**One-liner:** Competition is for losers. Create monopolies.
**Key insight:** Don't compete on features or price. Create something so different that you have no competition.

### 17. The Arc of Technology (September 2008)
**One-liner:** Technology moves in cycles. We're in the deployment phase.
**Key insight:** The 2000s were the installation phase. The 2010s would be the deployment phase—when technology spreads through the economy.

### 18. How to Be an Effective Executive (October 2008)
**One-liner:** Focus on the three things that matter.
**Key insight:** Effective executives: (1) set clear priorities, (2) communicate them relentlessly, (3) execute with speed.

### 19. The Case for the Virtual Company (November 2008)
**One-liner:** You don't need an office.
**Key insight:** Distributed teams can be more effective than co-located. The tools are good enough. Talent is global.

### 20. The Future of Hollywood (December 2008)
**One-liner:** Hollywood will be disrupted by the Internet.
**Key insight:** Distribution is no longer a moat. Content creators can reach audiences directly. Middlemen will be disintermediated.

### 21. How to Invest in Startups (January 2009)
**One-liner:** Invest in people, not ideas.
**Key insight:** Ideas change. Markets change. Great founders adapt. Bet on the person.

### 22. The Power of Platforms (February 2009)
**One-liner:** Platforms beat products.
**Key insight:** Products have linear value. Platforms have network effects. Build platforms when you can.

### 23. How to Think About the Future (March 2009)
**One-liner:** The future is already here—it's just not evenly distributed.
**Key insight:** Look at what the best people are doing today. That's the future in 5 years.

### 24. The Case for Starting a Company Now (April 2009)
**One-liner:** The best time to start a company is during a downturn.
**Key insight:** Recessions clear the field. Talent is available. Costs are lower. Competition is distracted.

### 25. Goodbye, Pmarca (May 2009)
**One-liner:** I'm stopping the blog to focus on my new venture.
**Key insight:** Andreessen announced the end of Pmarca to focus on what would become Andreessen Horowitz.

## Themes Across the Blog

### 1. Product-Market Fit Is Everything
The single most important concept in startups. Nothing else matters until you have it.

### 2. People Matter More Than Ideas
Great founders adapt. Mediocre founders fail even with good ideas.

### 3. Startups Are Hard
Most fail. Don't do it unless you have to.

### 4. Competition Is for Losers
Create monopolies. Differentiate so much you have no direct competition.

### 5. Technology Is Deflationary
Technology makes things cheaper, better, faster. This is good for society.

### 6. Optimism Is Warranted
Despite setbacks, technology advances. The future is bright.

---

## Impact

The Pmarca blog:
- Coined or popularized key startup terminology
- Influenced a generation of founders and VCs
- Established Andreessen as a thought leader
- Created the foundation for Andreessen Horowitz's philosophy

Many of these essays are still cited today, over 15 years later.
