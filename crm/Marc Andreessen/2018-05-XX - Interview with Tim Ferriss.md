# Marc Andreessen - Interview with Tim Ferriss (2018)

**Date:** May 2018  
**Source:** The Tim Ferriss Show  
**Interviewer:** Tim Ferriss

---

## About This Interview

A wide-ranging conversation covering Andreessen's reading habits, decision-making frameworks, views on AI, crypto, and the future of technology.

---

## The Interview

### On Reading

**Tim Ferriss:** You're known for reading constantly. What's your system?

**Marc Andreessen:** I read about 50-100 books a year. I read broadly—history, science fiction, biographies, technical books.

**Ferriss:** How do you retain what you read?

**Andreessen:** I don't worry about retention. I trust that the good stuff sticks. If I forget something, it probably wasn't important.

I also re-read. The best books I read multiple times. You get different things from them at different points in your life.

### On Decision Making

**Ferriss:** How do you make big decisions?

**Andreessen:** I use a few frameworks:

**1. Regret minimization**
Project forward to age 80. What will you regret more—doing this or not doing this?

**2. Inversion**
Instead of asking "how do I succeed?", ask "how do I avoid failure?" Then don't do those things.

**3. Second and third order thinking**
What are the consequences of the consequences? Most people think one step ahead. I try to think three.

### On Artificial Intelligence

**Ferriss:** How do you think about AI?

**Andreessen:** AI is the most important technology of our lifetime. It will transform everything.

There are two camps:
- **AI utopians:** AI will solve all our problems
- **AI doomers:** AI will destroy humanity

I think both are wrong. AI is a tool. Like fire, electricity, the computer. It will be used for good and bad. The key is to develop it responsibly.

**Ferriss:** What about job displacement?

**Andreessen:** Every technology displaces jobs. The printing press put scribes out of work. The tractor put farmhands out of work. But new jobs are created.

The question is: can we retrain people fast enough? That's the real challenge.

### On Cryptocurrency

**Ferriss:** You're a big crypto believer. Why?

**Andreessen:** Crypto is the next computing platform. The Internet enabled information to flow freely. Crypto enables value to flow freely.

Think about it: you can send an email to anyone, anywhere, instantly, for free. But you can't send money that way. Crypto fixes that.

**Ferriss:** What about the volatility?

**Andreessen:** Early Internet stocks were volatile too. Amazon dropped 90% in 2000. The technology and the price are different things.

### On Silicon Valley

**Ferriss:** Is Silicon Valley still the center of innovation?

**Andreessen:** Yes, but it's changing. Remote work is real. Talent is global. We're seeing great companies started everywhere.

But the Valley still has the density, the capital, the culture. It's the center of gravity.

### On Failure

**Ferriss:** You've had failures. Loudcloud, for example.

**Andreessen:** Loudcloud was too early. We were trying to do cloud computing in 1999. The technology wasn't ready. The market wasn't ready.

But we learned. We pivoted to Opsware, sold to HP for $1.6 billion. Failure is data.

### On Work-Life Balance

**Ferriss:** How do you balance work and life?

**Andreessen:** I don't believe in balance. I believe in intensity.

When I'm working, I'm all in. 80-100 hour weeks. When I'm not working, I'm all in on family, friends, reading.

I don't do the 40-hour week thing. That's neither great work nor great life.

### On the Future

**Ferriss:** What are you most excited about?

**Andreessen:** Three things:

**1. AI**
The next 10 years will be wild. AI will augment human capabilities in ways we can't imagine.

**2. Crypto/Web3**
A new layer of the Internet. Programmable money, decentralized applications, new business models.

**3. Biotech**
We're on the verge of understanding biology at the code level. CRISPR, mRNA vaccines—this is just the beginning.

## Key Insights

### 1. Reading Broadly
Andreessen reads 50-100 books a year across disciplines. Trusts that good ideas stick.

### 2. Decision Frameworks
Regret minimization, inversion, second-order thinking.

### 3. AI as Tool
Not utopian or doomer. AI is a powerful tool that will transform everything.

### 4. Crypto as Platform
The next computing platform—enabling value to flow like information.

### 5. Intensity Over Balance
All-in on work or all-in on life. Not the 40-hour middle ground.
