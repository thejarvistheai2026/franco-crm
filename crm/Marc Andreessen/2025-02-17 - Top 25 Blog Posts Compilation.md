# Marc Andreessen - Top 25 Blog Posts Compilation

**Compiled:** 2025-02-17  
**Source:** Pmarca.com (2007-2009) + pmarchive.com + a16z.com essays

---

## The Top 25 Posts

### 1. Why Software Is Eating the World (August 2011)
**One-liner:** Every industry is becoming a software industry.
**Key insight:** Software companies are taking over large swathes of the economy. This is still early days.
**Impact:** The defining essay of the 2010s tech boom. Legitimized software as transformative.
**Link:** <https://a16z.com/why-software-is-eating-the-world/>

---

### 2. The Only Thing That Matters (June 2007)
**One-liner:** In a startup, only product-market fit matters.
**Key insight:** Before PMF, nothing else matters—not team, not business model, not funding. After PMF, everything gets easier.
**Impact:** Coined/popularized "product-market fit" as we use it today.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/guide_to_startups_part4.html>

---

### 3. Why Not to Do a Startup (June 2007)
**One-liner:** Startups are brutally hard. Don't do one unless you have to.
**Key insight:** The only valid reason is that you can't not do it. Money, fame, flexibility—bad reasons.
**Impact:** Brutal honesty about startup difficulty.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/why_not_to_do_a_startup.html>

---

### 4. How to Hire the Best People (July 2007)
**One-liner:** Hiring is the most important thing you do. Do it obsessively.
**Key insight:** Spend 25% of your time hiring. Never compromise. One bad hire can destroy a startup.
**Impact:** Changed how startups think about recruiting.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/how_to_hire_the_best_people.html>

---

### 5. The Moby Dick Theory of Big Companies (August 2007)
**One-liner:** Big companies are like whales—slow and easily avoided.
**Key insight:** Startups worry too much about competition from big companies. They can't move fast enough.
**Impact:** Gave startups confidence to compete with incumbents.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/the_moby_dick_theory_of_big_companies.html>

---

### 6. Retaining Great People (September 2007)
**One-liner:** Once you hire great people, you have to keep them.
**Key insight:** Great people have options. Give them interesting work, autonomy, and ownership.
**Impact:** Early articulation of modern talent retention.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/retaining_great_people.html>

---

### 7. How to Create a Truly Great Company (November 2007)
**One-liner:** Great companies are built on product, people, and culture.
**Key insight:** Product comes first. Then hire great people. Culture emerges from those two.
**Impact:** Simple framework for company building.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/how_to_create_a_truly_great_company.html>

---

### 8. The Truth About VCs (January 2008)
**One-liner:** VCs are not your friends. They're investors.
**Key insight:** VCs have their own incentives—fund size, timeline, portfolio construction. Understand them.
**Impact:** Demystified venture capital for founders.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/the_truth_about_vcs.html>

---

### 9. How to Raise Money (February 2008)
**One-liner:** Raising money is a sales process. Treat it like one.
**Key insight:** Create competition among investors. Have a timeline. Know your numbers. Close fast.
**Impact:** Practical guide to fundraising.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/how_to_raise_money.html>

---

### 10. The Guide to Personal Productivity (March 2008)
**One-liner:** Don't keep a schedule. Don't answer the phone.
**Key insight:** Protect your time ruthlessly. Batch communication. Work in long blocks.
**Impact:** Influenced productivity thinking in tech.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/the_guide_to_personal_productivity.html>

---

### 11. The Psychology of Entrepreneurial Misjudgment (October 2007)
**One-liner:** Entrepreneurs are prone to specific cognitive biases.
**Key insight:** Overconfidence, confirmation bias, and sunk cost fallacy are particularly dangerous.
**Impact:** Applied behavioral economics to entrepreneurship.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/the_psychology_of_entrepreneurial_misjudgment.html>

---

### 12. How to Think About Competition (August 2008)
**One-liner:** Competition is for losers. Create monopolies.
**Key insight:** Don't compete on features or price. Create something so different you have no competition.
**Impact:** Influenced Thiel's "Zero to One" thinking.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/how_to_think_about_competition.html>

---

### 13. The Arc of Technology (September 2008)
**One-liner:** Technology moves in cycles. We're in the deployment phase.
**Key insight:** The 2000s were installation. The 2010s would be deployment—technology spreading through the economy.
**Impact:** Predicted the 2010s tech boom.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/the_arc_of_technology.html>

---

### 14. The Case for Optimism (May 2008)
**One-liner:** Things are getting better, not worse.
**Key insight:** Despite the financial crisis, technology advances. Innovation continues. The future is bright.
**Impact:** Counter-cyclical optimism during the crash.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/the_case_for_optimism.html>

---

### 15. How to Be an Effective Executive (October 2008)
**One-liner:** Focus on the three things that matter.
**Key insight:** Effective executives: (1) set clear priorities, (2) communicate them relentlessly, (3) execute with speed.
**Impact:** Simple framework for leadership.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/how_to_be_an_effective_executive.html>

---

### 16. The Case for the Virtual Company (November 2008)
**One-liner:** You don't need an office.
**Key insight:** Distributed teams can be more effective. The tools are good enough. Talent is global.
**Impact:** Early articulation of remote work benefits.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/the_case_for_the_virtual_company.html>

---

### 17. How to Invest in Startups (January 2009)
**One-liner:** Invest in people, not ideas.
**Key insight:** Ideas change. Markets change. Great founders adapt. Bet on the person.
**Impact:** Influenced angel investing approach.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/how_to_invest_in_startups.html>

---

### 18. The Power of Platforms (February 2009)
**One-liner:** Platforms beat products.
**Key insight:** Products have linear value. Platforms have network effects. Build platforms when you can.
**Impact:** Influenced platform strategy thinking.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/the_power_of_platforms.html>

---

### 19. When the VCs Say "No" (December 2007)
**One-liner:** Rejection is the default. Learn from it.
**Key insight:** Most VCs will say no. That doesn't mean your idea is bad. Wrong timing, wrong investor, or need to prove more.
**Impact:** Helped founders handle rejection.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/when_the_vcs_say_no.html>

---

### 20. The Future of the News Business (July 2008)
**One-liner:** Newspapers are dying. News is not.
**Key insight:** The business model is broken, not the need for information. New models will emerge.
**Impact:** Predicted media transformation.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/the_future_of_the_news_business.html>

---

### 21. How to Think About the Future (March 2009)
**One-liner:** The future is already here—it's just not evenly distributed.
**Key insight:** Look at what the best people are doing today. That's the future in 5 years.
**Impact:** Framework for predicting trends.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/how_to_think_about_the_future.html>

---

### 22. The Case for Starting a Company Now (April 2009)
**One-liner:** The best time to start a company is during a downturn.
**Key insight:** Recessions clear the field. Talent is available. Costs are lower. Competition is distracted.
**Impact:** Encouraged founders during the financial crisis.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/the_case_for_starting_a_company_now.html>

---

### 23. How to Be a Genius (June 2008)
**One-liner:** Genius is 1% inspiration, 99% perspiration—and the right environment.
**Key insight:** Genius requires: deep domain knowledge, time to think, freedom to explore, high bar for quality.
**Impact:** Demystified genius as learnable.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/how_to_be_a_genius.html>

---

### 24. Why I Love Twitter (April 2008)
**One-liner:** Twitter is the pulse of the Internet.
**Key insight:** Real-time information flow changes everything. Twitter is the new RSS, chat, and status update.
**Impact:** Early validation of Twitter's importance.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/why_i_love_twitter.html>

---

### 25. Goodbye, Pmarca (May 2009)
**One-liner:** I'm stopping the blog to focus on my new venture.
**Key insight:** Andreessen announced the end of Pmarca to focus on what would become Andreessen Horowitz.
**Impact:** End of an era. Beginning of a16z.
**Link:** <https://pmarchive.com/goodbye_pmarca.html>

## Themes Across All Posts

### 1. Product-Market Fit Is Everything
The single most important concept in startups. Nothing else matters until you have it.

### 2. People Matter More Than Ideas
Great founders adapt. Mediocre founders fail even with good ideas.

### 3. Startups Are Hard
Most fail. Don't do it unless you have to.

### 4. Competition Is for Losers
Create monopolies through differentiation.

### 5. Technology Is Deflationary
Technology makes things cheaper, better, faster. Good for society.

### 6. Optimism Is Warranted
Despite setbacks, technology advances. The future is bright.

### 7. Time Management Is Critical
Protect your time. Work in long blocks. Avoid meetings.

### 8. Culture Is Everything
At scale, culture becomes your operating system.

## Why Marc Andreessen Matters

- Co-created Mosaic, the first widely-used web browser
- Co-founded Netscape, kickstarted the dot-com era
- Founded Loudcloud/Opsware, sold for $1.6B
- Co-founded Andreessen Horowitz, one of the top VC firms
- Coined "product-market fit" and "software is eating the world"
- Wrote 100+ essays that shaped startup thinking
- Early investor in Facebook, Twitter, Airbnb, Coinbase, and many more
