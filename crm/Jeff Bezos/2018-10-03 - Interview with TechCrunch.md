# Jeff Bezos - Interview with TechCrunch (2018)

**Date:** October 3, 2018  
**Source:** TechCrunch Disrupt SF
**Interviewer:** Jon Russell  
**Location:** San Francisco

---

## About This Interview

A discussion focused on Amazon's expansion, international strategy, and the company's approach to entering new markets and sectors.

---

## The Interview

### On International Expansion

**Jon Russell:** Amazon started in the US but is now global. What's the key to international success?

**Jeff Bezos:** It's not one-size-fits-all. You have to adapt locally while keeping the core principles. Customer obsession is universal. But how you execute changes by market.

**Russell:** Give me an example.

**Bezos:** India is mobile-first. Entirely different than the US. We had to build completely different experiences, payment systems, logistics networks.

### On Entering New Categories

**Russell:** Amazon started as a bookstore. Now you sell everything. How do you decide what to enter next?

**Bezos:** We look for customer pain points. If customers are frustrated with an experience, that's an opportunity. Healthcare, grocery — these are categories where customers aren't happy.

### On Whole Foods

**Russell:** You bought Whole Foods for $13.7 billion. Why?

**Bezos:** Physical retail is important. And Whole Foods has great brand, great products, great locations. It's a complement to Amazon, not a replacement.

### On Competition

**Russell:** Everyone is competing with Amazon now. Walmart, Google, Microsoft...

**Bezos:** Good. Competition keeps us sharp. But our focus is customers, not competitors. If we obsess over competitors, we react. If we obsess over customers, we lead.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Local Adaptation
Global principles with local execution.

### 2. Customer Pain Points
New categories identified by customer dissatisfaction, not market size.

### 3. Complementary Strategy
Whole Foods as physical complement to digital.
