# Jeff Bezos - Interview with Charlie Rose (2010)

**Date:** November 18, 2010  
**Source:** The Charlie Rose Show, PBS  
**Interviewer:** Charlie Rose  
**Location:** New York

---

## About This Interview

Bezos discussed the Kindle, Amazon's culture of innovation, and his vision for the company's future. This was during a pivotal time as Amazon was expanding beyond retail into devices and cloud computing.

---

## The Interview

### On the Kindle

**Charlie Rose:** The Kindle is a huge success. Did you expect this?

**Jeff Bezos:** We believed in it, but you never know. The Kindle isn't a device, Charlie. It's a service. It's about instant access to any book, any time, anywhere.

**Rose:** Some say it will kill physical books.

**Bezos:** I don't think so. Different formats serve different purposes. What we see is that Kindle owners read more books, not fewer. That's good for readers and good for authors.

### On Innovation

**Rose:** How do you maintain a culture of innovation?

**Bezos:** We have a culture of experimentation. We try things, and many of them fail. But that's okay. Failure and invention are inseparable twins.

**Rose:** Give me an example of a failure.

**Bezos:** We've had many. Auctions didn't work. We tried a search engine. Some things just don't catch on. But you have to be willing to fail if you want to invent.

### On Customer Focus

**Rose:** You've said you're customer-obsessed, not competitor-obsessed.

**Bezos:** Absolutely. Competitors can change. Customers won't. If you focus on competitors, you're always reacting. If you focus on customers, you're leading.

**Rose:** What's the hardest part of being customer-obsessed?

**Bezos:** Being patient. Some investments take years to pay off. You have to have conviction and stay the course.

### On Amazon Prime

**Rose:** Prime was another bold move. Free two-day shipping?

**Bezos:** It seemed crazy. Everyone thought it would bankrupt us. But we believed customers would love it, and they do. Prime members buy more, shop more frequently, and are more loyal.

### On Blue Origin

**Rose:** Tell me about Blue Origin. Space?

**Bezos:** I'm passionate about space. It's the next frontier. Blue Origin's mission is to lower the cost of space flight so that we can have millions of people living and working in space.

**Rose:** That's a long-term vision.

**Bezos:** Very long-term. Decades. Maybe longer. But important things take time.

## Key Insights

### 1. Kindle as Service
Positioned Kindle as access to content, not just a hardware device.

### 2. Failure and Invention
"Failure and invention are inseparable twins" — classic Bezos philosophy.

### 3. Customer vs. Competitor Focus
Explained why focusing on customers is more sustainable than competitor-watching.

### 4. Patience with Returns
Emphasized that customer-focused investments take time to mature.

### 5. Long-Term Space Vision
Blue Origin represents his longest-term bet — space colonization.
