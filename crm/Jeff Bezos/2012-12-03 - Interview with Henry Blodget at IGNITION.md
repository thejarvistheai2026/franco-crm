# Jeff Bezos - Interview with Henry Blodget at IGNITION (2012)

**Date:** December 3, 2012  
**Source:** Business Insider IGNITION Conference  
**Interviewer:** Henry Blodget  
**Location:** New York

---

## About This Interview

A revealing conversation about Amazon's approach to competition, margin compression, and the long-term vision that frustrates short-term investors.

---

## The Interview

### On Low Margins

**Henry Blodget:** Investors constantly worry about your margins. Why don't you just raise prices?

**Jeff Bezos:** Our approach is to have low prices, not high margins. We want to be Earth's most customer-centric company. Low prices are part of that.

**Blodget:** But shareholders want profits.

**Bezos:** Shareholders who want short-term profits should probably invest elsewhere. We're building something for the long term.

### The Customer Obsession Philosophy

**Blodget:** You've said "start with the customer and work backwards." Why is this so important?

**Bezos:** Because it's durable. Competitors can copy your features. They can undercut your prices temporarily. But they can't copy your customer obsession. That's culture.

**Blodget:** How do you maintain this culture at scale?

**Bezos:** It's hard. You have to reinforce it constantly. We have leadership principles. We hire for it. We reward it. And sometimes, we have to part ways with people who aren't aligned.

### On Amazon's "Secret"

**Blodget:** What's Amazon's secret sauce?

**Bezos:** There is no secret. It's just customer obsession, long-term thinking, and willingness to be misunderstood for long periods of time.

**Blodget:** Misunderstood?

**Bezos:** For years, people said Amazon would never be profitable. They said we were a ponzi scheme. They said the model didn't work. We kept building anyway.

### On Innovation

**Blodget:** What's the next big thing for Amazon?

**Bezos:** I don't know. And if I did, I wouldn't tell you. We have thousands of people working on new ideas. Most will fail. A few will be big.

**Blodget:** Prime, AWS, Kindle...

**Bezos:** Those all seemed like crazy ideas when we started them. People questioned Prime: "How can you afford free shipping?" People questioned AWS: "You're not a tech company." People questioned Kindle: "Physical books are fine."

### On Patience

**Blodget:** What's the one thing most businesses get wrong?

**Bezos:** Impatience. They want results too quickly. They cut long-term investments to meet quarterly expectations. They optimize for the short term and sacrifice the long term.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Low Prices Over High Margins
Amazon's business model intentionally accepts lower margins for market share and customer loyalty.

### 2. Willingness to Be Misunderstood
Long-term thinking requires enduring skepticism and criticism.

### 3. Customer Obsession as Culture
Culture is the competitive advantage that can't be easily copied.

### 4. Impatience as Fatal Flaw
Short-term optimization destroys long-term value.
