# Jeff Bezos - Interview with Axel Springer (2016)

**Date:** May 24, 2016  
**Source:** Axel Springer Award Ceremony
**Interviewer:** Mathias Döpfner  
**Location:** Berlin, Germany

---

## About This Interview

A wide-ranging interview covering Amazon's culture, leadership principles, day one philosophy, and his views on competition and long-term thinking.

---

## The Interview

### On "Day One"

**Mathias Döpfner:** You've said "It's always Day One" at Amazon. What does that mean?

**Jeff Bezos:** Day One is a mindset. Day Two is stasis, followed by irrelevance, followed by excruciating decline, followed by death. That's why it's always Day One.

**Döpfner:** How do you maintain Day One?

**Bezos:** Customer obsession, a skeptical view of proxies, eager adoption of external trends, high-velocity decision making.

### On Proxies

**Döpfner:** What do you mean by "skeptical view of proxies"?

**Bezos:** As organizations get bigger, process becomes a proxy for results. You see it in big companies — people focus on following process rather than getting the right answer. That's dangerous.

### On Leadership Principles

**Döpfner:** Tell me about Amazon's leadership principles.

**Bezos:** We have 14 of them. They're not just decorations — we hire by them, we promote by them, we manage by them. Things like "Customer Obsession," "Ownership," "Bias for Action," "Frugality."

**Döpfner:** Which is most important?

**Bezos:** Customer Obsession. That's the starting point.

### On Work-Life Balance

**Döpfner:** You're known for working hard. Do you believe in work-life balance?

**Bezos:** I think the phrase is misleading. I prefer "work-life harmony." If I'm energized at work, I'm a better husband, a better father. If I'm happy at home, I'm a better CEO.

### On Long-Term Thinking

**Döpfner:** Why are you so focused on the long term?

**Bezos:** Most business is optimized for the short term — this quarter, this year. That leaves a lot of opportunities for anyone willing to think in longer time horizons. You can do things that others can't or won't do.

## Key Insights

### 1. Day One Philosophy
Day One mindset keeps the company from becoming stagnant and bureaucratic.

### 2. Process as Proxy
Large organizations risk substituting process for results — a critical trap to avoid.

### 3. Leadership Principles as Operating System
14 principles that guide hiring, promotion, and management.

### 4. Work-Life Harmony
Reframed work-life balance as harmony, where energy flows between both.
