## Interview Transcript

### The Pace of AI Progress

**Interviewer:** How fast is AI actually progressing?

**Jensen Huang:** Faster than people realize. Let me give you concrete numbers.

In 2012, AlexNet required 262 petaflop-days of computing. In 2020, GPT-3 required 3,100 petaflop-days — about 12x more.

But the compute we're deploying now is growing exponentially. And not just in chips — across the entire stack: algorithms, systems, networking, software.

We're seeing one or two orders of magnitude improvement per year, measured per dollar and per watt. The rate of progress is unlike anything in computing history.

Moore's Law gave us ~2x every two years. We're seeing 10x per year in AI effective compute. That's unprecedented.

### Global AI Competitiveness

**Interviewer:** China has made significant AI advances. Are you concerned?

**Jensen Huang:** China is doing excellent work. DeepSeek shows that Chinese research labs are producing world-class results.

But let's be clear: Huawei is a formidable competitor. They're a world-class technology company. The AI researchers in China — walk the halls at Anthropic, OpenAI, DeepMind, NVIDIA. You'll find Chinese researchers everywhere. These are not Chinese AI researchers; they are world-class AI researchers who happen to be Chinese.

Attempting to hold back Chinese AI development through export controls misunderstands the nature of scientific progress and the global distribution of talent.

### The AI Diffusion Framework

**Interviewer:** You've been vocal about the AI diffusion framework. Why?

**Jensen Huang:** Because the current framework is exactly wrong if the goal is American leadership.

Here's why: AI is a full-stack technology. Leadership requires leading at every layer — chips, systems, infrastructure, models, and applications. You can't sacrifice the chip layer to protect the model layer. It's nonsensical.

If we restrict American chip sales to China, where 50% of the world's AI researchers live and work, we cede that market to competitors. We weaken our own ecosystem. We reduce the flywheel that makes American technology dominant.

The framework should accelerate adoption of American technology globally. That's how we maintain leadership.

### AI Factories in the Middle East

**Interviewer:** You recently announced major AI infrastructure deals in the Middle East. What's the significance?

**Jensen Huang:** Two things make this region perfect for AI factories:

First, abundant energy. Training modern AI models requires enormous compute, which requires substantial power. These countries have energy.

Second, scarce labor. These countries are constrained by population size. AI offers something transformative: digital labor. AI agents. AI-assisted productivity. They can leapfrog traditional industrialization.

I met with leaders in Saudi Arabia and Abu Dhabi. They're passionate, forward-thinking, and understand the technology deeply. They're building massive AI infrastructure — data centers, talent pipelines, startup ecosystems.

This isn't just buying chips. They're building an AI economy from the ground up.

### Enterprise Pragmatism

**Interviewer:** Enterprises are interested in AI but want ROI. What do you tell them?

**Jensen Huang:** I love that. Enterprises should be pragmatic.

The best way to get ROI from AI is to start with high-value, low-risk use cases. Customer service automation. Code generation for developers. Document processing. These have clear, measurable benefits.

Start there. Learn. Build expertise. Then expand to more transformational applications.

The key is customization. Generic models are great, but custom models trained on your data, your workflows — that's where the real value is.

NVIDIA's AI foundry model lets enterprises do exactly this. Train, customize, deploy their own AI with full control.

### The Supply Chain Innovation

**Interviewer:** Where's the innovation happening beyond the chip itself?

**Jensen Huang:** Everywhere. That's what people miss.

Yes, Blackwell has 208 billion transistors. But that's just one component.

We invented new packaging technology. New networking (NVLink). New precision formats (FP4). New compression. New software optimizations. New system architectures.

The chip is maybe 1/10 of the innovation. The other 9/10 is the full system stack.

And we're not doing this alone. Our ecosystem — partners, customers, researchers — pushes the whole industry forward.
