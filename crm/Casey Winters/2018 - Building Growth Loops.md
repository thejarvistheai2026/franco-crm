# Casey Winters - Building Growth Loops

**Date:** 2018  
**Source:** Reforge Growth Series

---

## About This Talk

Casey Winters explains why funnels are outdated and growth loops are the modern approach to sustainable growth. Loops create compounding effects that don't require constant marketing spend.

---

## The Talk

### Funnels vs. Loops

**Casey Winters:** Traditional marketing uses funnels. You pour money in the top, users come out the bottom. It's linear: spend X, get Y users.

**The problem:** Funnels don't compound. You have to keep spending to keep growing.

**Loops are different.**

Loops are systems where output becomes input. Every user you acquire helps you acquire more users. Every piece of content you create creates more engagement which creates more content.

### Types of Growth Loops

**Winters:** There are three main types:

**1. Viral Loop**
- Users bring users
- The product itself encourages sharing or invites
- Examples: Dropbox referral, Instagram tagging, Zoom invites

**2. Content Loop**
- Users create content → Content attracts users → Users create more content
- Examples: Reddit, Yelp, YouTube, Pinterest

**3. Paid Loop**
- Revenue → Ad spend → Users → Revenue
- Works if LTV > CAC
- Examples: Most DTC brands, many SaaS companies

### The Viral Loop

**Winters:** Viral loops work when:
- The product has inherent sharing (collaboration, invites)
- There's value to both inviter and invitee
- The viral coefficient > 1

**Viral Coefficient = Invites sent × Conversion rate**

If coefficient > 1, you grow exponentially without marketing spend.

If coefficient < 1, you still get viral "lift" but need other channels.

### The Content Loop

**Winters:** Content loops are especially powerful:
- Users create content (reviews, posts, photos, answers)
- Content gets indexed by Google
- Search brings new users
- New users create content

**This is how Pinterest grew:**
- Users pin content
- Pins get indexed
- Search brings users
- Users pin more content

**This is how Uber grew:**
- Drivers join, provide rides
- Riders rate drivers
- Better-rated drivers get more rides
- More riders join
- More drivers join

### The Paid Loop

**Winters:** Paid loops are the default but least efficient:
- You spend on ads
- You get users
- Users generate revenue
- You reinvest in ads

**The key question:** Is LTV > CAC?

If yes, you can scale. If no, you die.

### Pinterest's Loops

**Winters:** Pinterest had multiple loops working together:

**Content Loop:**
- User pins → content grows → SEO traffic → new users

**Viral Loop:**
- Users invite friends → friends see pins → join

**Engagement Loop:**
- More pins → better recommendations → more engagement → more pins

These loops compounded. Each made the others stronger.

### How to Build a Loop

**Winters:** Steps to building a growth loop:

**1. Map your user journey**
- How do users find you?
- How do they sign up?
- What do they do first?
- What makes them stay?
- What makes them bring others?

**2. Identify natural sharing moments**
- When does a user benefit from bringing others?
- When are users most excited about the product?

**3. Design for compounding**
- How does today create value for tomorrow?
- What accumulates? Content? Users? Data?

**4. Measure the loop**
- What's your viral coefficient?
- What's your content production rate?
- What's your SEO growth?

### Loop Metrics

**Winters:** Key metrics by loop type:

**Viral:**
- Invites per user
- Invite conversion rate
- Viral coefficient
- Time to invite

**Content:**
- Content production rate
- Content quality
- Organic traffic growth
- Content-to-user ratio

**Paid:**
- CAC (Customer Acquisition Cost)
- LTV (Lifetime Value)
- Payback period
- LTV/CAC ratio

## Key Insights

### 1. Loops > Funnels
Loops compound. Funnels don't.

### 2. Multiple Loops Compound
Having more than one loop makes each stronger.

### 3. Viral Is Rare
Most products don't have true viral loops. Don't force it.

### 4. Content Loops Take Time
SEO takes months or years. Patience required.

### 5. Paid Loops Are Valid
Not every product can be viral. Paid loops work if unit economics are good.

## Application

### For Startups
Map your growth loop before you optimize tactics. What system will give you compounding growth?

### For Growth Teams
Don't just optimize the funnel. Build and measure loops.

### For Investors
Ask: "What's your growth loop?" Not "what are your marketing channels?"
