# Casey Winters - How to Build an SEO Growth Engine

**Date:** 2017  
**Source:** Reforge Essay

---

## About This Essay

A practical guide to building SEO as a sustainable growth channel. Casey draws from Pinterest's massive SEO success while avoiding the common mistakes companies make with SEO.

---

## The Essay

### SEO Is Not Magic

**Casey Winters:** People treat SEO like magic. "If we just publish content, Google will send us traffic."

SEO doesn't work that way. SEO is a system. It requires infrastructure, strategy, and patience.

### The SEO Flywheel

**Winters:** Sustainable SEO looks like this:

**Content → Indexing → Ranking → Clicks → Users → More Content**

Each step requires work:
- Content: Something worth indexing
- Indexing: Google knows you exist
- Ranking: You show up in results
- Clicks: People actually visit
- Users: People find value
- More Content: They create or engage with content

If any step breaks, the flywheel stops.

### What Pinterest Did Right

**Winters:** Pinterest is one of the largest SEO success stories. Here's why it worked:

**1. User-Generated Content at Scale**
Users created millions of pins. Each pin was a page. Each page could rank.

**2. Structured Data**
We marked up content so Google understood it: images, titles, descriptions, topics.

**3. Clean URLs**
Every pin had a unique, clean URL. Google could crawl and index everything.

**4. Internal Linking**
Pinterest's grid structure created massive internal linking. Every page linked to related content.

**5. Fresh Content**
Users kept pinning. Google likes fresh content. Pinterest always had new pages.

### Common SEO Mistakes

**Winters:** Here's what companies do wrong:

**1. Thin Content**
Publishing 1000 garbage pages doesn't help. Quality matters more than quantity.

**2. Ignoring Technical SEO**
If Google can't crawl your site, nothing else matters. Fix your basics.

**3. No Content Strategy**
"We'll just blog." About what? For whom? Without strategy, you get random content.

**4. Impatience**
SEO takes months or years. Companies quit too early.

**5. Not Building Links**
Links still matter. But build them naturally, not through manipulation.

### The Content Strategy

**Winters:** Good SEO content answers:
- What are people searching for?
- Can we answer it better than competitors?
- Will the answer be satisfying?

**At Pinterest, we had two content types:**

**1. User Content (Pins)**
Millions of images, each with context: title, description, board, user.

**2. Editorial Content**
Collections, guides, trends. Curated content around search trends.

Both ranked, but user content scaled infinitely.

### Technical SEO Basics

**Winters:** Before you worry about content, get these right:

**1. Crawlability**
- Clean site architecture
- XML sitemaps
- No orphan pages

**2. Page Speed**
- Fast load times (especially on mobile)
- Core Web Vitals

**3. Mobile-Friendly**
- Responsive design
- Mobile-first indexing

**4. Structured Data**
- Schema markup
- Rich snippets

**5. Canonical URLs**
- No duplicate content
- Clear canonical signals

### The Long Game

**Winters:** SEO is a long game. At Pinterest:

- Year 1: Set up infrastructure, minimal traffic
- Year 2: Content grew, traffic started climbing
- Year 3: Compounding effects, significant traffic
- Year 4+: Massive organic traffic, lower CAC

Most companies quit in year 1.

### SEO for Marketplaces

**Winters:** Marketplaces (like Airbnb, Eventbrite) have SEO advantages:

**1. Inventory-Based Content**
Every listing is content. Cities, events, categories.

**2. Natural Link Building**
Listers share their listings. Creates backlinks.

**3. Transactional Intent**
People searching to buy/book have high intent.

**4. Local SEO**
Local events, local businesses rank for geo queries.

But marketplaces also have challenges:
- Duplicate content (same event on multiple sites)
- Content quality varies (user-generated)
- Seasonality

### Measuring SEO

**Winters:** Key SEO metrics:

**Leading indicators:**
- Indexed pages
- Crawl errors
- Page speed

**Lagging indicators:**
- Organic traffic
- Keyword rankings
- Click-through rate

**Business metrics:**
- Leads/signups from organic
- Revenue from organic
- CAC reduction from organic

### The Team

**Winters:** SEO requires:

**Technical SEO:** Engineer who understands search infrastructure

**Content:** Writers who can create search-optimized content

**Analytics:** Someone who can track and measure

**Product:** Features that generate or optimize content

It's cross-functional. No one person can do it all.

## Application

### For Startups
Don't rely on SEO initially. But start building it in year 1-2.

### For Scale-Ups
If you have user-generated content, you have an SEO advantage. Optimize it.

### For Growth Teams
SEO should be part of your channel mix. Not your only channel, not ignorable.
