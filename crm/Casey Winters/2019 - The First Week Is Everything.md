# Casey Winters - The First Week Is Everything

**Date:** 2019  
**Source:** Reforge Essay

---

## About This Essay

Casey explains why the first week of user experience is disproportionately important for long-term retention—and how to optimize it.

---

## The Essay

### The Data Pattern

**Casey Winters:** Every product I've analyzed shows the same pattern: users who are retained long-term behave differently in their first week than users who churn.

At Pinterest, users who became long-term active users:
- Took specific actions in their first session
- Returned within 24 hours
- Completed their profile
- Discovered personalized content

Users who churned:
- Bounced after viewing one page
- Never returned
- Saw generic, non-personalized content
- Never felt the "aha moment"

### Why the First Week Matters

**Winters:** The first week is when users decide:
- Is this for me?
- Is this useful?
- Should I remember to come back?

If they don't get value quickly, they never will. Attention is scarce. Users move on.

### The Retention Correlation

**Winters:** We analyzed Pinterest data:

**Users who returned day 2:**
- 40% were still active at month 1
- 25% were still active at month 6

**Users who didn't return day 2:**
- 5% were active at month 1
- 1% were active at month 6

**Day 2 return rate predicted long-term retention.**

This isn't unique to Pinterest. Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter—the same pattern holds.

### The Aha Moment

**Winters:** Users need an "aha moment"—the point where they understand the product's value.

At Pinterest, the aha moment was:
- **Saving a pin** — You find something and save it
- **Creating a board** — You organize your saves
- **Seeing personalized recommendations** — The content feels like "for you"

Users who hit all three in their first week had much higher retention.

### Optimizing the First Week

**Winters:** To improve first-week retention:

**1. Identify the aha moment**
- What action correlates with retention?
- What do your best users do early?

**2. Design onboarding around it**
- Guide users to that action
- Remove friction to get there
- Don't overwhelm with features

**3. Bring them back**
- Email or push notification within 24 hours
- Remind them why they signed up
- Show them something new

**4. Celebrate progress**
- "You saved your first pin!"
- Gamify early milestones
- Create positive reinforcement

### The Pinterest First Week

**Winters:** Here's how we redesigned Pinterest onboarding:

**Before:
- Sign up
- See generic feed
- Get lost
- Never return

**After:**
- Sign up
- Choose interests (personalization)
- See personalized feed immediately
- Guided tour of key actions
- Email 24 hours later with fresh content
- Onboarding drip over 7 days

**Result:** +20% activation rate, +15% D7 retention.

### Common First Week Mistakes

**Winters:** I see these constantly:

**1. Feature overload**
- Showing every feature at once
- Users don't know what to do

**2. Generic experience**
- Same experience for everyone
- No personalization

**3. No clear CTA**
- Users don't know what action to take
- No guidance

**4. Delayed value**
- "Set up your profile" before showing content
- Users bounce before seeing value

**5. No follow-up**
- User signs up, never hear from product again
- Opportunity lost

### The Email Drip

**Winters:** The first-week email sequence:

**Day 1:** Welcome + next step
"Thanks for joining! Here's what to do next..."

**Day 2:** Educational
"Here's how [feature] works..."

**Day 3:** Social proof
"See how others use [product]..."

**Day 4:** Value reminder
"You haven't saved anything yet. Here's why people save..."

**Day 7:** Re-engagement
"Did you know you can do [X]? Try it now..."

Not spam. Useful, targeted, helping users get value.

### Activation vs. Retention

**Winters:** Activation is the first step. Retention is the outcome.

Users can activate (sign up, take first action) but not retain (never come back).

The first week bridges that gap. It turns activation into habit.

### Measuring First Week Success

**Winters:** Key first-week metrics:

**1. Activation rate**
% who complete the aha moment action

**2. D1 retention**
% who return the next day

**3. D7 retention**
% who return within 7 days

**4. First-week actions**
- Feed views, saves, messages, etc.
- Correlation with long-term value

**5. Cohort curves**
Do retention curves flatten after week 1?

---

## Key Insights

### 1. First Week Predicts Everything
Users who succeed in week 1 retain long-term.

### 2. The Aha Moment
Identify the action that correlates with retention. Design for it.

### 3. Day 2 Return Is Critical
The 24-hour return rate is the best predictor of retention.

### 4. Follow Up
Don't let users forget you exist. Email, notifications—but valuable ones.

### 5. Bridge Activation to Retention
The first week turns "interested" into "habitual."
