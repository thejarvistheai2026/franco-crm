## PART 1: TOP 30 BLOG POSTS

### 1. The Product Builder's True Journey
**Published:** December 15, 2025  
**Source:** shreyasdoshi.substack.com  
**One-liner:** Product builders live two lives — the first life of "just building" and the second life of understanding the psychological trap of attachment and shackles.  
**Key Insights:**
- First life: "Just build, move fast, ship" — naive approach
- Second life begins when you realize you get attached to & shackled by everything you build
- "Average PM" needs discipline to compensate; "exceptional PM" may not (by definition)
- The mantras that work for exceptional PMs can harm average PMs
- Applies to founders, engineers, designers — not just PMs

### 2. Is Writing Really Thinking
**Published:** December 27, 2025  
**Source:** shreyasdoshi.substack.com  
**One-liner:** "Writing is thinking" contains truth, but it's routinely overextended — understand when writing aids vs. replaces thinking.  
**Key Insights:**
- Writing helps crystallize thinking, but doesn't replace it
- People with weak internal thinking become dependent on external writing
- "Writing is thinking" has become cargo culted
- Internal clarity can exist without external writing
- Balance: writing as tool, not requirement

### 3. Why Products Fail
**Published:** December 17, 2025  
**Source:** shreyasdoshi.substack.com  
**One-liner:** Real product work requires correct thinking — the common causes of product failure stem from flawed decision-making.  
**Key Insights:**
- Most product failures aren't execution problems; they're thinking problems
- Teams optimize for wrong things (vanity metrics, short-term gains)
- Wisdom > Knowledge > Information
- Focus on "what truly matters" vs. what feels urgent
- Product failure often stems from founder/PM psychology

### 4. Time Management: A Deep Dive
**Published:** January 3, 2026  
**Source:** shreyasdoshi.substack.com  
**One-liner:** The LNO Framework (Leverage/Neutral/Overhead) for managing time and decisions at different leadership levels.  
**Key Insights:**
- **L**everage: High-impact, strategic decisions (CEO/VP level)
- **N**eutral: Run-of-the-mill decisions (Sr PM level)
- **O**verhead: Low-value decisions (Jr PM level)
- As you advance, your decision types should shift
- Most people spend too much time on Overhead at senior levels

### 17. Destined to Fail: A B2B Product Management Story
**Published:** April 2021  
**Source:** shreyasdoshi.substack.com

**Key Insights:**

### 18. The 3 Levels of Product Management
**Published:** 2024  
**Source:** The Knowledge Project Podcast  
**One-liner:** From execution to strategy to vision — the progression of product management mastery.  
**Key Insights:**
- **Level 1 (Execution):** Ship features, manage backlog, execute roadmap
- **Level 2 (Strategy):** Set direction, prioritize initiatives, align stakeholders
- **Level 3 (Vision):** Define future, shape company strategy, create market
- Most PMs stay at Level 1
- Level 3 requires wisdom and deep thinking

### 19. The 7 Biases of Product Teams
**Published:** 2020  
**Source:** Plato Elevate2020  
**One-liner:** Cognitive biases that derail product teams — and how to combat them.  
**Key Insights:**
- **Confirmation Bias:** Seeking evidence that confirms existing beliefs
- **Sunk Cost Fallacy:** Continuing due to past investment
- **Planning Fallacy:** Underestimating time/complexity
- **Optimism Bias:** Overestimating success probability
- **Groupthink:** Conforming to group consensus
- **Survivorship Bias:** Learning only from successes
- **Availability Heuristic:** Overweighting recent/available information

### 22. Customer Problem Stack Ranking (CPSR)
**Published:** 2021  
**Source:** shreyasdoshi.substack.com / Twitter  
**One-liner:** The framework for B2B product prioritization — solving the #1 customer problem, not the one that aligns with your solution.  
**Key Insights:**
- Have customers rank their problems 1-N
- Solve #1 problem, even if it's not what you want to build
- Forces product-market alignment
- Prevents solution-in-search-of-problem
- Changes sales conversation from "buy my product" to "what's your #1 problem?"

**Key Insights:**

### 24. The LNO Framework
**Published:** 2023-2024  
**Source:** shreyasdoshi.substack.com / Lenny's Podcast  
**One-liner:** Leverage, Neutral, Overhead — categorizing decisions and tasks by impact level.  
**Key Insights:**
- **Leverage:** High-impact, strategic (10x returns) — focus here
- **Neutral:** Necessary but not transformative (1x returns) — automate/delegate
- **Overhead:** Low value, administrative (-1x) — eliminate or minimize
- Senior leaders should spend 80%+ time on Leverage
- Most people spend too much on Neutral/Overhead

### 25. Founder Mode Analysis
**Published:** September 2024  
**Source:** Interview / Substack  
**One-liner:** The right and wrong way to activate Founder Mode — what Paul Graham got right and what leaders miss.  
**Key Insights:**
- Founder Mode ≠ micromanagement
- Right: Deep engagement with details when it matters
- Wrong: Disempowering capable teams
- "Founder mode" vs. "Manager mode" false dichotomy
- Great leaders know when to switch modes

### 26. Evaluating vs. Measuring Progress
**Published:** 2024  
**Source:** The Knowledge Project Podcast  
**One-liner:** The difference between measuring (lagging) and evaluating (leading) — why great PMs do the latter.  
**Key Insights:**
- **Measuring:** Quantitative, backward-looking metrics
- **Evaluating:** Qualitative, forward-looking judgment
- Most companies over-index on measuring
- Evaluating requires wisdom and experience
- Great PMs evaluate more than they measure

**Key Insights:**

- Clear decision-making authority matters
- Data helps but rarely decides for you
- Product sense is the tie-breaker
- Speed of decision often matters more than perfect decision

### 28. Good Teams vs. Bad Teams
**Published:** 2024  
**Source:** The Knowledge Project Podcast  
**One-liner:** The observable differences between high-performing and struggling product teams.  
**Key Insights:**
- Good teams: psychological safety, clear ownership, aligned incentives
- Bad teams: politics, unclear roles, misaligned metrics
- Team quality matters more than individual talent
- Good teams learn faster
- Leadership creates environment for good teams

### 29. Product Sense & Strategy Frameworks
**Published:** 2024  
**Source:** Courses / Substack  
**One-liner:** Developing product sense — the intuitive judgment that separates good from great PMs.  
**Key Insights:**
- Product sense can be developed
- Requires deep customer understanding + pattern recognition
- Strategy = making choices about what NOT to do
- Product sense = knowing what will work before data proves it
- Built through experience, reflection, and deliberate practice

### 30. The Art of Product Management
**Published:** August 2022  
**Source:** Interview (YouTube)  
**One-liner:** Product management as craft — combining science and art in building great products.

- Copying frameworks without context fails
- "Art" is pattern recognition from thousands of examples

## Mental Models & Frameworks

### Shreyas's Core Frameworks
- **The 3 Levels of PM:** Execution → Strategy → Vision
- **LNO Framework:** Leverage (10x) / Neutral (1x) / Overhead (-1x)
- **The 2 Lives:** First life (just build) vs. Second life (attachment awareness)
- **CPSR:** Customer Problem Stack Ranking for B2B prioritization
- **Evaluating vs. Measuring:** Leading (evaluating) vs. lagging (measuring) indicators

### Key Definitions
- **Wisdom = Discerning what truly matters**
- **Happiness = Reality × Expectations**
- **Product Sense** = Pattern recognition from accumulated experience
- **Average PM** = needs discipline frameworks
- **Exceptional PM** = may not need explicit frameworks

### The 7 Biases of Product Teams
1. Confirmation Bias
2. Sunk Cost Fallacy
3. Planning Fallacy
4. Optimism Bias
5. Groupthink
6. Survivorship Bias
7. Availability Heuristic

### Decision Making
- **Breaking Ties:** Authority + Product Sense + Speed
- **Product Decisions:** Context > Frameworks
- **Strategic Decisions:** Wisdom over data

### Team Dynamics
- **Good Teams:** Psychological safety, clear ownership, aligned incentives
- **Bad Teams:** Politics, unclear roles, misaligned metrics
- **Big Egos:** Route around, don't confront

### Time Management
- **Leverage Activities:** Strategic decisions, relationships, vision setting
- **Neutral Activities:** Meetings, status updates, process
- **Overhead Activities:** Administrative, low-value coordination
