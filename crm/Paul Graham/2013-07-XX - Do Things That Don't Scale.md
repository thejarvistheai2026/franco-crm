# Paul Graham - Do Things That Don't Scale

**Date:** July 2013  
**Source:** Y Combinator Blog

---

## About This Essay

Perhaps PG's most influential essay. The counterintuitive advice that successful startups often begin with unscalable manual work.

---

## The Essay

### The Common Mistake

**Paul Graham:** One of the most common types of advice we give at Y Combinator is to do things that don't scale.

A lot of would-be founders believe that startups either take off or don't. You build something, make it available, and if you've made a better mousetrap, people beat a path to your door.

That's not how startups work.

### Examples

**Stripe:**
When Stripe was founded, the Collison brothers didn't just build an API and wait. They personally onboarded every user. If you wanted to use Stripe, Patrick or John would call you and walk you through it.

This gave them two advantages:
- They learned exactly what was confusing
- They built relationships with their early users

**Airbnb:**
When Airbnb was struggling to grow, Brian Chesky went to New York and personally photographed hosts' apartments. He stayed at their places. He improved their listings one by one.

That doesn't scale. But it got them their first 40 users.

### Why It Works

**Graham:** There are two reasons why startups should do things that don't scale:

1. **To learn:** Manual processes teach you what your users actually need.
2. **To delight:** Early users should love you, not just like you.

### The Hand-Wringing Phase

**Graham:** There's a phase at the beginning of every startup where you're doing things that won't scale.

Don't worry. That's normal.

- Manually recruiting users? Normal.
- Personally serving customers? Normal.
- Writing individual onboarding emails? Normal.

### When to Scale

**Graham:** Eventually, you have to scale. But not yet.

Don't automate until you've manually done something enough times to know what you're automating. Don't hire until the work is crushing you.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Manual First, Automated Later
Build the unscalable version first. Learn. Then automate.

### 2. Personal Delight
Early users should experience something extraordinary, not just adequate.

### 3. Learn by Doing
Manual processes teach you what users actually need.

### 4. The Hand-Wringing Phase is Normal
Every successful startup goes through a period of unscalable work.

### 5. Don't Hire Too Early
Wait until the work is actually crushing you.

---

## Famous Quotes

- "Do things that don't scale."
- "There are two reasons why startups should do things that don't scale: to learn, and to delight."
- "Don't automate until you've manually done something enough times."

---

## Impact

This essay became a foundational YC concept, cited by countless founders. The advice to manually onboard users, personally serve customers, and delay automation became standard startup practice.
