# Paul Graham - Startup School 2005 Keynote

**Date:** April 2005  
**Source:** First Y Combinator Startup School  
**Location:** Harvard University

---

## About This Talk

The inaugural Y Combinator Startup School, where Paul Graham laid out the foundations of what would become the YC philosophy.

---

## The Talk

### Why Start a Startup

**Paul Graham:** Why would anyone start a startup? It's hard. It consumes your life. The odds are against you.

The answer is: because you can. In our time, it's possible for a small group of people to build something that changes the world. That wasn't always true.

### The Power of Software

**Graham:** Software is leverage. A programmer can build something that serves millions of people. That's power.

In the old economy, you needed capital, factories, distribution. In the software economy, you need a laptop and determination.

### The Idea

**Graham:** Don't worry about the business model initially. Worry about building something people want. If you have users, you can figure out the business model later.

Google didn't have a business model for years. They just built something great and figured out money later.

### The Cofounder Question

**Graham:** Single founders are at a disadvantage. Startups are too hard for one person. You need someone to share the burden with.

Also, startups pivot. Your initial idea is probably wrong. You need someone to go through that with.

### The YC Model

**Graham:** Here's what YC does. We give you a small amount of money. You move to Cambridge for the summer. We work with you intensely. At the end, you present to investors.

It's like a startup accelerator. We compress years of startup education into three months.

### What We Look For

**Graham:** We look for smart people who can build things. We don't care about your credentials. We care about what you've built.

Show us your side projects. Show us your GitHub. That's what matters.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Software is Leverage
Small teams can have massive impact through software.

### 2. Users First, Business Model Second
Build something people want, figure out money later.

### 3. Cofounders are Critical
Single founders face structural disadvantages.

### 4. What You've Built > Credentials
Side projects and code matter more than degrees.
