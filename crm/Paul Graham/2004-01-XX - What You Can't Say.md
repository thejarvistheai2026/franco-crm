# Paul Graham - What You Can't Say

**Date:** January 2004  
**Source:** Paul Graham's Essays

---

## About This Essay

A philosophical essay on moral fashions, intellectual taboos, and the importance of being able to think thoughts that are currently unspeakable.

---

## The Essay

### Moral Fashion

**Paul Graham:** Have you ever seen an old photo of yourself and been embarrassed by how you looked? That's how we should feel about our past opinions.

Every era has its moral fashions. Things that are considered obviously true and obviously important—until they're not.

### Galaxies

**Graham:** Think about it this way: Moral fashions are like galaxies. We're always in the middle of one, and it looks like the whole universe. But there are other galaxies.

The things you can't say in your galaxy might be common sense in another.

### The Taboo

**Graham:** In every society, there are things you can't say. Not because they're false. Because they're taboo.

The question is: Which of these taboos are worth keeping? And which are just moral fashion?

### Finding What You Can't Say

**Graham:** How do you find what you can't say in your own time?

1. Look for things that get people in trouble for saying
2. Notice what makes you flinch
3. Ask: Is this actually true, or just taboo?

### The Cost of Taboos

**Graham:** Taboos are expensive. They prevent you from thinking clearly about important topics.

If you can't question certain ideas, you're at their mercy.

### Why It Matters for Startups

**Graham:** For startup founders, this matters because:

- The best startup ideas often look obviously wrong at first
- If you can't think thoughts that are currently unspeakable, you'll miss big opportunities
- The future is often heresy in the present

### Be Agnostic

**Graham:** The solution is to treat your current beliefs as hypotheses, not certainties.

Be intellectually agnostic. Hold ideas lightly enough that you can examine them.

## Key Insights

### 1. Moral Fashions Change
Today's obvious truths will look strange to future generations.

### 2. Taboos Prevent Thought
What you can't say, you can't think clearly about.

### 3. Find Your Galaxy's Edges
Notice what makes you uncomfortable—you might be near a taboo.

### 4. Hold Beliefs Lightly
Treat beliefs as hypotheses, not certainties.

### 5. Startups Need Heresy
The best ideas often look wrong initially.

## Impact

This essay sparked significant discussion about free speech, intellectual freedom, and the importance of questioning prevailing orthodoxies. It's one of PG's most philosophically ambitious essays.
