# Paul Graham - Interview with Jason Calacanis

**Date:** 2015  
**Source:** This Week in Startups (Episode 500)

---

## About This Interview

Jason Calacanis interviews Paul Graham about YC's evolution, what makes great founders, and startup advice.

---

## The Interview

### On YC's Evolution

**Jason Calacanis:** Paul, YC has changed a lot. What's the biggest difference?

**Paul Graham:** When I started, YC was the three of us—me, Robert Morris, and Jessica Livingston—funding 8 companies in Cambridge. Now it's a global machine funding hundreds of companies per batch.

The philosophy is the same. The scale is completely different.

### On Great Founders

**Calacanis:** What makes a great founder?

**Graham:** Determination is the most important thing. Startups are so hard that you have to be incredibly determined to survive.

But there's also another quality: founders need to be smart. Not just IQ smart, but resourceful smart. They need to be able to figure things out.

**Calacanis:** Can you teach determination?

**Graham:** Not really. You can select for it. But if someone doesn't have it, you can't install it.

### On Idea Validation

**Calacanis:** How do you know if an idea is good?

**Graham:** You don't. Not at the beginning. You have to build it and see.

The best approach is: Build the minimum version as fast as possible. Get it in front of users. Learn. Iterate.

**Calacanis:** What's the biggest mistake?

**Graham:** Waiting too long to launch. Founders want to perfect the product before showing it to anyone. That's backwards.

### On Competition

**Calacanis:** Should founders worry about competition?

**Graham:** Worry about making something great. If you do that, competition doesn't matter.

Most "competitors" aren't really competing. They're just similar companies. Real competition is rare.

### On Fundraising

**Calacanis:** Advice for fundraising?

**Graham:** Raise money when you can, not when you need to. And raise the amount you need plus a buffer.

Also: Don't optimize for valuation. Optimize for the right investors. A good investor adds more value than the extra dilution costs.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Determination is Key
The most important founder quality can't be taught, only selected for.

### 2. Launch Fast
Don't wait for perfection. Build the minimum version and learn.

### 3. Don't Overthink Competition
Competition is less of a threat than most founders think.

### 4. Raise When You Can
Don't wait until you're desperate.
