# Paul Graham - How to Get Startup Ideas

**Date:** 2012  
**Source:** Y Combinator Blog/Essay Talk

---

## About This Essay

One of PG's most-read essays, outlining how to find good startup ideas rather than trying to "think of them."

---

## The Essay

### The Wrong Way

**Paul Graham:** The way to get startup ideas is not to try to think of them. That's the wrong approach.

When people try to think of startup ideas, they come up with things like "a social network for pets" or "Uber for laundry." These are bad ideas because they're artificially constructed.

### The Right Way

**Graham:** The way to get good startup ideas is to become the sort of person who has them.

What does that mean? It means:
- Learn a lot about something that matters
- Notice problems and inefficiencies
- Be at the leading edge of a field

### Live in the Future

**Graham:** Good startup ideas exist in the future. To see them, you need to be living there already.

How do you live in the future? By working on interesting problems. By being around other people who are doing it. By paying attention to what annoys you.

### Problems You Have

**Graham:** The best startup ideas are problems you personally have. Because:
- You'll know the problem is real
- You'll be passionate about solving it
- You'll have domain expertise

If you don't have the problem yourself, you're building in the dark.

### Seize the Opportunity

**Graham:** When you notice a problem you have, ask: Could this be a startup?

Most problems aren't startup-sized. But some are.

### School is Not the Answer

**Graham:** Don't look for startup ideas in class. School is designed to teach you established knowledge, not to help you find new ideas.

New ideas come from working on things, not from studying.

### The Schlep Filter

**Graham:** Most people unconsciously avoid hard problems. They apply a "schlep filter"—dismissing ideas because they seem like too much work.

Good ideas often seem like too much work. That's why they haven't been done.

### Unsexy Ideas

**Graham:** The best ideas are unsexy. If an idea sounds exciting, probably too many people are working on it.

Look for ideas that seem tedious. That's where the opportunity is.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Don't Try to Think of Ideas
Let ideas emerge from your experience and expertise.

### 2. Live in the Future
Be on the cutting edge where new problems and opportunities exist.

### 3. Solve Your Own Problems
You'll have natural advantages if you personally experience the problem.

### 4. The Schlep Filter
Don't dismiss ideas just because they seem hard.

### 5. Unsexy is Good
Boring-sounding ideas face less competition.

## Famous Quotes

- "The way to get startup ideas is not to try to think of them."
- "Live in the future, then build what's missing."
- "The best startup ideas are problems you personally have."
- "The best ideas are unsexy."
