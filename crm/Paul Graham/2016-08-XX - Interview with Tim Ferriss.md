# Paul Graham - Interview with Tim Ferriss

**Date:** August 2016  
**Source:** The Tim Ferriss Show  
**Interviewer:** Tim Ferriss

---

## About This Interview

Paul Graham discusses his writing process, decision-making frameworks, life philosophy, and what he's learned from funding hundreds of startups.

---

## The Interview

### On Maker's Schedule vs Manager's Schedule

**Tim Ferriss:** You wrote about maker's schedule and manager's schedule. Can you explain?

**Paul Graham:** Makers—programmers, writers, artists—need long blocks of uninterrupted time. Even a single meeting can destroy a whole day.

Managers live by the clock. They schedule in hour increments. If you try to mix these, the maker loses.

**Ferriss:** How do you protect your maker time?

**Graham:** I have a rule: no meetings before 5pm. I write in the mornings. Meetings in the afternoon.

### On Decision Making

**Ferriss:** How do you make big decisions?

**Graham:** I try to think about what I'll regret. What would future me wish I had done?

Also, I think about reversible vs irreversible decisions. Most decisions are reversible. Don't overthink those. Save your energy for the irreversible ones.

### On Y Combinator Evolution

**Ferriss:** How has YC changed since you started?

**Graham:** It's much bigger now. When I started, we funded 8 companies a batch. Now it's hundreds.

Also, I stepped away in 2014. Sam Altman took over. Since then it's grown even more—batch sizes, locations, programs.

**Ferriss:** Do you miss running it?

**Graham:** I miss parts of it. But I also like having my time back. YC is a machine now. It doesn't need me.

### On Life Philosophy

**Ferriss:** What have you learned about life?

**Graham:** Do things that compound. Knowledge, relationships, reputation—these compound over time.

Also, don't optimize for status. Optimize for interestingness. An interesting life is better than a prestigious one.

### On Advice He'd Give Himself

**Ferriss:** What would you tell your 20-year-old self?

**Graham:** Learn more things. Don't specialize too early. The most interesting opportunities come at the intersection of fields.

Also, don't worry so much. Most things you're worried about won't matter.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Maker's Schedule
Protect long blocks of uninterrupted time for deep work.

### 2. Reversible vs Irreversible Decisions
Distinguish between decisions you can undo and decisions you can't.

### 3. Compound Interest Applies to Everything
Knowledge, relationships, reputation—all compound.

### 4. Interestingness > Prestige
Live an interesting life, not just a successful-looking one.
