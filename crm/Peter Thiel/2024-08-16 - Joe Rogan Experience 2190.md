## Key Insights

### Peter Thiel as Emperor of the World

If Peter Thiel were emperor:
- Figure out how to have smaller governments
- Increase the age for social security
- Means-test social security
- Dial back other government benefits

Social security was originally designed to be more like a pension system, not a welfare system.

### The California Resource Curse

California just increased its tax rate to 14.4%. More people are leaving California than ever before, but state revenues continue to increase.

California is kind of like Saudi Arabia:
- Distortions of the mind: crazy religion (Wokism in CA and Wahhabism in Saudi Arabia)
- Wealth: Oil field in Saudi and Big tech companies in CA
- Bloated government sector
- Distorted real estate markets
- Wealth is redistributed in bizarre and distorted ways

Four of the nine companies with market capitalizations over a trillion dollars are based in California.

### Zero-Tax States

States with no tax and no cities: Alaska, Wyoming, South Dakota, and New Hampshire.

Peter's top two choices amongst zero-tax, relatively more libertarian states:
1. **Nashville, Tennessee**
2. **Miami, Florida**

**On Austin and Texas:**
- Houston is an oil town
- Dallas has an inferiority complex to Los Angeles and New York City
- Austin is a government and college town and a wannabe hipster San Francisco town

### The Rise of Miami and Austin

Since 2020, it has gotten much more expensive to move to places like Miami and Austin. Austin's rise was linked to California, Miami's rise was linked to New York.

The finance world is less networked, so it's easier for a New York based private equity shop to move to Miami and seamlessly continue operations. This dynamic may be less true for the tech industry.

### Crypto vs. AI

> "If we say that crypto is libertarian, can we also say that AI is communist?"

It feels like AI will be dominated by the Big Tech companies based in the Bay Area. There is something about AI that makes it very centralized.

### Peter Thiel Predicts the Future of AI

No one has any idea where AI is headed.

**Books mentioned:**
- **Superintelligence** by Nick Bostrom — Explores potential risks of AI surpassing human intelligence
- **AI Superpowers** by Kai-Fu Lee — Examines AI competition between US and China

ChatGPT is the version of AI that people talked about from 1950-2010; it is a form of AI that passes the Turing Test.

AI in 2024 is like the internet in 1999:
- It was clear the internet would rearrange society
- But a massive bubble formed
- It took about 15-20 years for internet companies to function properly

It may take two decades to figure out how to best apply current breakthroughs in AI.

### The Great Stagnation

A paradox of living through an era of relative tech stagnation is that when something does happen, we do not know how to process it. The creation/discovery of Bitcoin is an example — it was underappreciated for the first 10 years.

Over the last several decades, we have made significant progress in the space of bits, not less so in atoms. The world of atoms has stagnated while the world of electrons has rapidly progressed.

**Subtract the digital screens from society... What has changed?**

### Fake Sciences

Adding the word "Science" to a discipline creates dogmatism:
- Climate Science
- Political Science
- Social Science
- Computer Science

Actual science: Physics, Chemistry, Mathematics.

> "Computer science was a fake field for people who found electrical engineering or math way too hard."

### Nuclear Energy

The history of energy has trended towards more intense and dense forms: From wood → coal → oil → uranium.

It is regressive for this trend to go in the other direction. Installing solar panels all over the desert takes up more space and is a less dense form of energy capture.

Nuclear was supposed to be the energy mode of the 21st century. Nuclear energy was handicapped because it turned out to be very dual use (power plants and weapons), which made it dystopian and apocalyptic.

### Civilization, Myths, and Imitating Apes

Our civilization today is way more advanced than advanced civilizations in history. They probably didn't have nuclear weapons or spaceships.

The human ability to use language is a discontinuity in Darwin's theory of evolution. Humanity's ability to tell stories and myths differentiates us from primates.

From Aristotle's Poetics: "Man is the most imitative of animals and is also the most capable of learning."

Humans are giant imitation machines; to imitate is to ape.

### The CIA and MK-Ultra

**Book Recommendation:** *Chaos: Charles Manson, The CIA, and the Secret History of the Sixties* by Tom O'Neill.

MK-Ultra was a covert CIA program during the Cold War that sought to develop mind control techniques through experiments involving drugs, hypnosis, and other methods.

Today's government and deep state are shockingly less competent than they once were; it is not clear if this is good or bad for society on net.

### Declining Birth Rates

People have kids if other people have kids, and they stop having kids when other people stop having kids.

### Talk vs. Action

Talk is always a substitute for action; Ask if talk leads to action or substitutes for action.

## The Absolute State of Things

There are a lot of problems in the United States, but most places are doing a lot worse.

The relative outperformance of the U.S. and the absolute stagnation decline of the U.S. are related.

As a percentage of GDP, tax receipts that go towards the government deficit is at an all-time high. After a decade of zero-interest rate policy, interest rates have relatively increased.

We have a runaway deficit problem, and it will only get more expensive to service with higher rates. But if interest rates are kept low, then the debt increases.
