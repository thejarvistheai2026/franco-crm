## Top 25 Blog Posts Compilation

### 1. Tencent: The Ultimate Outsider (2020)
**Published:** August 17, 2020  
**Source:** notboring.co  
**One-liner:** The most important company that Westerners know the least about — the Chinese giant that combined Facebook, Nintendo, Shopify, Netflix, Spotify, Slack, and PayPal into one.  
**Key Insights:**
- Tencent = Facebook + Nintendo + Shopify + Netflix + Spotify + Slack + PayPal combined
- WeChat has 1.2B users who spend more time in-app than Americans on all social media combined
- Built $700+ investment portfolio (2nd only to Sequoia globally)
- Naspers' 2001 investment returned 10,000x — greatest venture return in history
- Gaming + social + payments + cloud infrastructure flywheel
- Most valuable company you've never analyzed

### 2. Stripe: The Internet's Most Undervalued Company (2020)
**Published:** August 31, 2020  
**Source:** notboring.co  
**One-liner:** At $36B, Stripe was a steal — analyzing the infrastructure layer powering internet commerce and why it could be worth more than Goldman Sachs.  
**Key Insights:**
- Mission: increase GDP of the internet
- Evolved from simple payments → full business operating system (Billing, Capital, Atlas, etc.)
- Infrastructure play = captures value from internet commerce growth without taking retail risk
- Stripe Press as strategic asset (attracts talent, inspires entrepreneurship)
- Comparison to public comps (PayPal, Adyen, Square) suggests massive undervaluation
- The "pick and shovel" play during the ecommerce gold rush

### 3. Knock Knock. Who's There? Opendoor (2020)
**Published:** September 21, 2020  
**Source:** notboring.co  
**One-liner:** The iBuyer that could become the Amazon of housing — analyzing the SPAC combining Chamath's vision with a $1.4T market opportunity.  
**Key Insights:**
- iBuying = instant offer on your home, solving liquidity problem
- Real estate transactions have massive friction; Opendoor removes it through pricing technology
- Network effects in local markets (more supply → better pricing → more demand)
- Bps for breakfast: culture of caring about basis points
- Path to vertical integration (buy → sell → finance → insure)
- TAM expansion: growing the market vs. just taking share

### 4. Software is Eating the Markets (2020)
**Published:** October 26, 2020  
**Source:** notboring.co  
**One-liner:** Marc Andreessen was right about software eating the world — now it's eating financial markets as retail investors reshape how assets are valued.  
**Key Insights:**
- Retail investors = 25% of volume (up from 10% historically)
- Social subsidy of investing: people invest for status/experience, not just returns
- New platforms blur investment/consumption/entertainment boundaries
- Alternative assets (sneakers, cards, crypto) become "fun" asset classes
- Low rates + stimulus + boredom + technology = permanent market structure shift
- Price decouples from pure fundamentals when assets serve multiple roles

### 5. Shopify and the Hard Thing About Easy Things (2020)
**Published:** August 10, 2020  
**One-liner:** If everyone can do something, there's no advantage in doing it, but you still have to do it just to keep up — the paradox of democratized tools.  
**Key Insights:**
- Shopify arms the rebels → everyone has same tools → no one has advantage
- DTC revolution: low barriers = high competition = compressed margins
- Value chain analysis: who captures value when everything modularizes?
- Casper/Blue Apron IPO failures vs. Dollar Shave Club success (supply scarcity)
- Porter's Five Forces + Value Chain frameworks applied to DTC
- Shopify wins when merchants compete; merchants lose unless differentiated

### 6. Oh Snap! (2020)
**Published:** June 15, 2020  
**One-liner:** Everyone left Snap for dead, but Evan Spiegel is executing on the Augmented Reality layer (Mirrorworld) play that could own the next computing platform.  
**Key Insights:**
- Gartner Hype Cycle: Snap went through trough of disillusionment
- AR as next platform shift (after mobile)
- Spectacles as long-term bet on AR glasses
- Snapchat as camera company, not social network
- "Mirrorworld" concept: digital overlay on physical world
- Stock up 92% after publication 🎯

### 7. When to Dig a Moat (2021)
**Published:** 2021  
**Source:** notboring.co  
**One-liner:** Timing matters — dig moats too early and you waste resources; too late and you're vulnerable. Framework for defensive strategy.  
**Key Insights:**
- Moat construction requires significant investment before you need it
- Premature optimization: building defenses before product-market fit
- Too late: competitors have already encircled you
- Network effects, scale economies, brand — when does each matter?
- Dynamic moat building: evolve defenses as company grows
- Case studies from companies who timed it right/wrong

### 8. In Defense of Strategy (2021)
**Published:** 2021  
**One-liner:** Against the "just build and see" ethos — why deliberate strategy still matters in fast-moving tech.  
**Key Insights:**
- Strategy = choosing what NOT to do (Buffett/Munger view)
- Emergent vs deliberate strategy balance
- Planning vs preparation distinction
- Strategic frameworks help make better decisions faster
- Not about rigid plans; about clear thinking under uncertainty

### 9. The Great Online Game (2021)
**Published:** 2021  
**Source:** notboring.co  
**One-liner:** The internet is the greatest game ever invented — here's how to play it well.  
**Key Insights:**
- Internet = multiplayer game with clear point systems (followers, status, money)
- Different games: Twitter = status game, Substack = monetization game
- Skills transfer between games
- Compound returns on reputation and audience
- Play long-term games with long-term people
- "Building in public" as strategy

### 10. The Vertical Integrator Series
**Published:** 2022-2024  
**Multi-part series:** Not Boring  
**One-liner:** Deep dives on companies that vertically integrate their value chains — Anduril, Varda, SpaceX, Tesla, and more.  
**Key Insights:**
- Vertical integration = owning more of the value chain
- Enables faster iteration, lower costs, better margins
- Defense applications (Anduril), space (Varda), transportation (Tesla)
- Hardware + software integration beats pure software in physical world
- Custom manufacturing beats shared infrastructure at scale
- Pattern recognition across frontier tech companies

### 11. Everything is Technology (2025)
**Published:** May 13, 2025  
**Source:** notboring.co  
**One-liner:** The addressable market for venture-backed tech will grow 10x as technology eats previously non-tech industries — Tesla, Uber, and the pattern of disruption.  
**Key Insights:**
- Technology eats world in two ways:
  1. Direct displacement (Tesla vs. auto incumbents)
  2. Market creation (Uber creating ride-hail from fragmented taxi market)
- $1B exits now ~85th percentile (not 99th) — fund math changes
- VC megafunds make sense when TAM expands by order of magnitude
- Examples: Ford vs. horses, Uber vs. taxis, Tesla vs. autos
- Speed of change underestimated — typically ~10 years for full transition

### 12. The Techno-Industrial Revolution (2022)
**Published:** 2022  
**Source:** notboring.co  
**One-liner:** We're entering a new industrial revolution led by technology companies building physical things — the age of atoms, not just bits.  
**Key Insights:**
- Transition from pure software to software-enabled hardware
- SpaceX, Tesla, Anduril, Hadrian — building real things with software speed
- Defense as first mover (bypassing traditional procurement)
- Manufacturing renaissance in America
- AI enables design → build → iterate cycles faster
- New industrial base = new economic paradigm

### 13. Better Tools, Bigger Companies (2022)
**Published:** 2022  
**Source:** notboring.co  
**One-liner:** As tools get better, the companies wielding them get bigger — the AI amplification thesis.  
**Key Insights:**
- Developer productivity tools → larger engineering teams possible
- AI tools → individual output increases → winner-take-more dynamics
- Tools compound: small advantages multiply across large organizations
- Historical pattern: railroads, electricity, computers each enabled larger organizations
- AI = biggest tool improvement yet → expect mega-companies

### 14. APIs All the Way Down (2021)
**Published:** 2021  
**Source:** notboring.co  
**One-liner:** The modern company builds on API infrastructure — modularization enables faster building but changes competitive dynamics.  
**Key Insights:**
- "There's no Atlas Shrugged for APIs" — invisible infrastructure
- Stripe, Twilio, AWS = foundational layers
- Tradeoff: speed to market vs. commoditization risk
- API-first companies = thin wrappers around infrastructure?
- Defensibility question: what happens when API layer raises prices?
- Bundle/rebundle dynamics in API economy

### 15. Super Apps (2020-2021)
**Published:** 2020-2021  
**Source:** notboring.co  
**One-liner:** The Super App is coming to the US — analyzing WeChat's model and who will build America's version.  
**Key Insights:**
- WeChat: messaging + payments + mini-programs + everything
- US attempts: Facebook failures, maybe Cash App, Shopify, or new player
- Context matters: China's fragmented infrastructure made Super Apps necessary
- US: better existing infrastructure, harder to consolidate
- Opportunity in enterprise (Slack/Teams) vs. consumer

### 16. The Unbundling of Excel (2021)

### 18. The Consumerization of Enterprise (2020)
**Published:** 2020  
**Source:** notboring.co

- Sales cycle compression
- Viral loops in B2B
- Slack, Zoom, Notion as exemplars

- Startups with capital can invest through downturn
- Pattern from 2008: great companies founded in crisis
- Current volatility = generational opportunity
- Strategy: survive first, then capitalize
