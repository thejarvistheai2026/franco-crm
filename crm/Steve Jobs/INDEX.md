## Core Ideas

### 1. Build What You Want to Use
- Start with your own needs
- Authenticity creates better products
- Don't do focus groups — be the focus group
- **See:** D5, Lost Interview, D3

### 2. Focus Is About Saying No
- Cut 70% of product lines to save Apple
- "No to a thousand good ideas, yes to a few great ones"
- Quality requires exclusion
- **See:** D3, Stanford

### 3. Taste Matters
- "Good taste is something you have or you don't"
- Excellence in software is 50-1 or 100-1
- Design is how it works, not just how it looks
- **See:** Lost Interview, D5

### 4. Death as Motivation
- Memento mori (remember you will die)
- "If today were the last day of my life..."
- External expectations fall away in face of mortality
- **See:** Stanford Commencement

### 5. Connecting the Dots
- Trust that the path will make sense later
- Curiosity without immediate purpose pays off
- Calligraphy → Mac typography → Windows typography
- **See:** Stanford Commencement

### 6. The Blue Box Mindset
- Small things can control big things
- "Little thing controlling giant thing"
- Smart products have outsized impact
- **See:** Lost Interview

### 7. Product Over Profit
- Start with great product, profit follows
- "We're just trying to make great products"
- Money is a lagging indicator
- **See:** All interviews

### 8. Great People Create Leverage
- A+ players vs. B players: 50-1 difference
- Small teams of excellent people
- Recruitment is the CEO's most important job
- **See:** Lost Interview

### 9. Craftsmanship Over Ideas
- Ideas are 10%; execution is 90%
- "5,000 things in your brain" fitting together
- Magic is in the iteration
- **See:** Lost Interview

### 10. Control the Experience
- "Not closed, but complete"
- Hardware + software + services = integration
- End-to-end control enables quality
- **See:** D3, D8
