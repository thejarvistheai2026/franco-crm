Jeanne combines deep product sensibilities with enterprise sales expertise. Her approach treats GTM as a product—designing the buying journey as intentionally as building any product feature. She's at the forefront of AI-powered sales transformation, pioneering the "GTM Engineer" role that uses AI agents to automate and scale sales processes.

### [00:00 - 07:53] Defining Go-to-Market Strategy in Today's Landscape

Lenny begins by asking Jeanne how she defines go-to-market.

> **Jeanne:** "GTM is any function that touches a customer or generates revenue—marketing, sales, post-sales roles like customer success and support, technical GTM roles like sales engineers, and partnerships. It's the entire lifecycle from prospect awareness to long-term, high-LTV relationships."

Jeanne explains that this broader lens helps teams map the jobs to be done across the customer journey, orchestrating them the same way they would orchestrate a product roadmap.

**Key Insights:**
- GTM is not just marketing and sales—it's any function that touches a customer or moves a dollar
- Think of GTM as an integrated lifecycle, not siloed activities
- The role of GTM is evolving toward holistic, "product-like" orchestration of the entire customer journey

**The Evolution of GTM:**
- Consumption-based models have driven more consultative GTM
- Focus has shifted to long-term customer outcomes and learning what customers actually need
- AI is accelerating this by enabling personalized, scalable experiences

### [07:54 - 18:17] The Rise of the GTM Engineer

Jeanne introduces one of her most impactful concepts: the **GTM Engineer**.

> **Jeanne:** "At Stripe in 2017, we tried to build a system that would automatically personalize outbound emails based on company attributes. It was audacious—and it failed to land. Today, with AI, that exact same approach works."

A defining story from Stripe anchors this discussion: **Rosalind**, the company-universe project. Stripe never fired off generic emails. Instead, they built a system with a row for every company and a column for every attribute that mattered to Stripe's sales motion. If a target ran a marketplace business model, Stripe Connect was the right segue; if it was simple payments, Vanilla was the fit.

The goal was a "Mad Libs" email: 80% fed by company attributes, leaving just the blanks for human touch. This was impossible in 2017 but is now viable with AI.

**The Modern GTM Engineer at Vercel:**
- Profile: Sales engineers or technically minded GTM people who can translate expert workflows into AI-enabled agents
- Process: Map every GTM workflow (outbound prospecting, inbound handling) into AI agents that can act, with humans in the loop
- Practical impact: **A single GTM engineer reduced 10 SDRs to 1 lead reviewer in just 6 weeks**

> **Jeanne:** "The lead agent cost around $1,000 a year to run on Vercel, versus the millions paid in salary for the SDR team it replaced. Lead-to-opportunity conversion rate stayed flat, but time-to-response plummeted and the number of touches dropped as the agent worked in real time."

### [18:18 - 29:04] Implementing AI in Sales: Human-in-the-Loop Approach

Jeanne discusses practical AI implementation for sales processes.

**Key Principles:**
- **Build vs. Buy:** Building your own agents isn't prohibitively expensive, and the payoff can be enormous
- **Internal First:** Start with internal workflows, then expand into generalized platforms
- **Human-in-the-Loop:** Agents handle routine tasks; humans review/approve critical outreach

**Tool Stack Mentioned:**
- **Gong:** For training data, transcripts, and enabling AI-enabled analysis
- **Internal Agents:** Lead agents, deal bots, lost bots
- **Slack Channels:** For real-time alerts and coaching

> **Jeanne:** "At a company racing to iterate—where new features launch every few weeks—this internal bot approach gives you a rhythm that traditional procurement cycles can't match."

### [29:05 - 38:59] Defining Roles and Scaling GTM with Engineering

Jeanne shares her perspective on what makes GTM teams effective at high-growth companies.

**Sales as R&D:**
> **Jeanne:** "The best salespeople and sales organizations are 50% revenue-generating and 50% R&D because they're out there talking to customers every day."

**The Product Management Litmus Test:**
> **Jeanne:** "If an account executive sits in front of ten engineers, those engineers should be able to tell within ten minutes that the person isn't a product manager. That depth of product understanding isn't a luxury—it's a prerequisite for credibility."

**Key Tactics:**
- Sales teams must "think like product managers" and serve as a bridge between revenue and R&D
- Deep product knowledge enables translating customer feedback into product signals
- GTM should inform pricing, roadmap, and go-to-market innovation

### [39:00 - 46:36] Leveraging AI for Deeper Insights and Internal Tooling

Jeanne introduces the concept of **deal bots** that analyze Gong transcripts to surface why deals are won or lost.

**The "Lost Bot" Example:**
The first big insight from their deal bot? A loss often isn't about price at all. It's about:
1. The absence of an economic buyer
2. Lack of a clear ROI argument

> **Jeanne:** "The deal bot reviewed every Slack thread, email, and Gong call and uncovered a recurring pattern: our best losses came from failing to quantify value in a way that the buyer could internalize."

**The Flywheel Effect:**
- Test a play
- Measure its impact
- Codify the learnings
- Sprint the improvements

> **Jeanne:** "It's not magic—it's a flywheel where we continuously learn and iterate."

### [46:37 - 57:54] GTM as a Product: Creating Unique Customer Experiences

Jeanne elaborates on treating GTM as a product—designing the buying journey with the same care as designing any product feature.

**Mapping the Customer Journey:**
- Map from awareness to renewal as you would map a product
- Aim for a highly human, personalized, and differentiating buying experience
- Add value at every touchpoint, even if the customer doesn't buy

**Whiteboarding as Sales Tactic:**
> **Jeanne:** "Use whiteboarding sessions and collaborative value diagrams to help customers design their own architecture or usage path. This informs whether you win—and if not, where you need to compete."

**Build vs. Buy in the AI Era:**
> **Jeanne:** "What failed seven years ago now works with AI. The calculus has fundamentally shifted. Tools that were impossible or too expensive to build can now be AI agents deployed in weeks."

### [57:55 - 1:09:30] Effective Segmentation Strategies

Jeanne shares her framework for effective segmentation—the foundation of any good GTM strategy.

**The Three-Attribute Heuristic:**
Use a two-axis framework with a third attribute layered in:

1. **Company Size (X-axis):** SMB / Mid-Market / Enterprise
2. **Growth Potential (Y-axis):** % YoY growth
3. **Business Model attributes:** B2B, B2C, B2B2C, Marketplace

> **Jeanne:** "For consumption-based businesses, high-growth customers carry more revenue potential—so growth potential on the Y-axis is crucial."

**The Vercel Segmentation Model:**
- **Crux rank:** A proxy for a site's traffic and reach
- **Workload type:** E-commerce vs. enterprise backend vs. marketing sites
- **Traffic tiers:** OpenAI was a prime example of a top-tier traffic site that pushes customers into enterprise because the buying journey must be more deliberate

**Alignment Principle:**
> **Jeanne:** "Ensure product teams understand segmentation (training all new hires on 'KYC' segmentation) so product, marketing, and sales speak the same language."

### [1:09:31 - 1:17:31] Building a Sales Org That Integrates with Product and Engineering

Jeanne discusses how to build sales organizations that product and engineering teams actually respect.

**The Failure Modes of Sales Leaders:**
1. Staying in individual contributor mindset instead of building systems
2. Failing to align with the product organization
3. Chasing any opportunity without clear strategy

**Hiring Philosophy:**
> **Jeanne:** "Hire a mix of sales experience and nontraditional backgrounds—consulting or banking—to bring analytical rigor and broader problem-solving to the GTM motion."

**Sales Philosophy:**
> **Jeanne:** "Sales is fundamentally about solving problems. People ultimately buy from you when they feel you're going to help them solve a legitimate problem that they have."

**Know When Not to Sell:**
> **Jeanne:** "Don't sell when the Internet can. For smaller companies and simple use cases, a salesperson isn't always necessary. A strong self-service engine can be more effective."

### [1:17:32 - 1:25:30] Pricing Strategies, Sales Compensation, and Hiring

Jeanne shares insights on pricing and compensation models.

**Pricing as Product Decision:**
- Map value to price
- Understand where costs are incurred
- Design structure so customers clearly see ROI

**Evolving Pricing:**
> **Jeanne:** "At Stripe, we had to kill a freemium strategy for Billing when it blunted ROI. At Vercel, we unbundled a SaaS-like price into an enterprise-focused SKU that can be bought self-serve online—this unlocked new growth paths."

**Compensation Evolution:**
- Balance performance with adaptability
- Be able to pivot incentives when new AI-enabled motions or product lines emerge

**The Hiring Hierarchy:**
- **SDRs:** Responsible for pipeline generation (inbound and outbound)
- **AEs:** Close deals
- **GTM Engineers:** Build AI-enabled workflows that replace traditional SDR functions

## Key Frameworks & Mental Models

### 1. **GTM as Product Framework**
**Definition:** Treat go-to-market as an integrated customer lifecycle that touches a customer from awareness to long-term value, designed with the same intentionality as building a product.

**When to Use:** When designing or restructuring your customer journey, hiring, or scaling GTM teams.

**Key Tactics:** Map the complete journey, add value at every touchpoint, whiteboard with prospects, design differentiated buying experiences.

---

### 2. **The GTM Engineer**
**Definition:** A sales engineer or technically-minded GTM person who translates human workflows into AI-enabled agents that can execute deterministic, scalable processes.

**When to Use:** For automating repetitive but high-leverage GTM workflows (prospecting, qualification, follow-up).

**Example:** Reducing 10 SDRs to 1 lead reviewer using AI agents while maintaining conversion rates.

---

### 3. **Three-Axis Segmentation Framework**
**Definition:** A segmentation approach using: (1) Company Size on X-axis, (2) Growth Potential on Y-axis, (3) Business Model attributes as the third dimension.

**When to Use:** For prioritizing accounts, designing plays, and aligning product/marketing/sales language.

**Key Insight:** For consumption-based businesses, growth potential is often more important than current size.

---

### 4. **Sales as R&D Framework**
**Definition:** The best sales organizations are 50% revenue-generating and 50% R&D, feeding customer insights back into product development.

**When to Use:** When building sales culture and evaluating sales performance.

**Litmus Test:** Can your AE sit with engineers and be indistinguishable from a PM for 10 minutes?

---

### 5. **Build vs. Buy in the AI Era**
**Definition:** The calculus has shifted—building internal AI agents is now often more cost-effective than buying SaaS tools, with faster iteration cycles.

**When to Use:** Evaluating tooling decisions for GTM stack.

**Cost Comparison:** $1,000/year for an AI agent vs. $500K+ for an SDR team performing the same function.

---

### 6. **Deal Bot Intelligence**
**Definition:** Using AI to analyze Gong transcripts, Slack threads, and emails to surface true win/loss reasons and enable real-time coaching.

**When to Use:** For improving sales process, training AEs, and identifying systematic failure modes.

**Key Insight:** Losses often attributed to price are actually failures to reach economic buyers or quantify ROI.

---

### 7. **The "Don't Sell When the Internet Can" Principle**
**Definition:** For smaller companies and simple use cases, a strong self-service engine is often more effective than human sales.

**When to Use:** Designing PLG motions and determining where to layer in human sales touch.

---

### 8. **The Platinum Rule of Management**
**Definition:** Manage others the way *they* need to be managed, not the way *you* personally like to be managed.

**When to Use:** Leading diverse teams with different working styles and preferences.

## Post-Interview Reflections

Jeanne DeWitt Grosser represents a new breed of sales leader—one who combines traditional enterprise sales expertise with deep product sensibilities and AI-native operational thinking. Her work at both Stripe and Vercel demonstrates that world-class GTM in 2026 requires:

1. **Technical fluency** - Understanding AI enough to build agents, not just buy tools
2. **Product-centricity** - Treating GTM as a designed experience, not just a conversion funnel
3. **Experimentation mindset** - Using data and AI to continuously test and iterate on processes
4. **Cross-functional respect** - Building sales teams that engineering and product teams actually trust

The emergence of the "GTM Engineer" role she pioneered may be one of the most significant shifts in how high-growth companies approach revenue generation in the coming years.
