## Guest Overview

**Jason Lemkin** is the founder and CEO of **SaaStr**, the world's largest community for B2B software founders, with over 500,000 members and annual conferences attracting 10,000+ attendees. He is a veteran SaaS investor who has deployed nearly **$200 million** into B2B startups, achieving almost **10x lifetime returns**.

Before SaaStr, Jason was a **two-time founder**, most notably as CEO of **EchoSign** (electronic signature platform), which he sold to **Adobe in 2011** for approximately $100 million. He has invested in 20+ SaaS companies including **Qualtrics, Algolia, Salesloft, and Pipedrive**.

**Why his perspective matters on AI sales:** In 2025, after his last salesperson quit on-site at SaaStr Annual, Jason made a radical decision: replace his entire go-to-market team with AI agents. He transformed SaaStr's sales org from ~10 humans (SDRs and AEs) to just **1.2 humans + 20 AI agents**—while maintaining the same revenue performance. He's living the future of sales and sharing real-world results, failures, and frameworks.

**Jason Lemkin describes his business:** We're a large community. We do 8 figures of revenue per year. It's work—we have 100 sponsors. There's a certain level where it becomes a lot of work. They just quit on-site."

**Jason's decision:**

So when I saw that a horizontal agent, not trained for sales, not trained for GTM, could close one deal... I just can't pay a junior SDR $150,000 a year to quit. I just can't. Criticize me, but I just couldn't do it one more time."

Jason's core insight is that you can train an AI agent on your best salesperson's emails and scripts. Once trained:

- It works 24/7 without quitting
- It scales infinitely because "software scales"

### [00:45:00 - 01:00:00] The Future of Sales Roles: Who Gets Displaced

**Jason's prediction on SDRs:**

> "The classic SDR—junior kid that is hired out of college to send emails and respond to inbound emails—we don't need them. **SDRs that knock on doors in a lot of industries aren't going to be displaced. The email-based cadence SDR will be 90% displaced by AI next year.**"

**On inbound lead qualification:**

> "BDRs—folks that qualify leads coming in, the 'contact me's' that we see—we have no need for them today. **They should be extinct next year.** There is no reason in the age of AI I have to hit 'contact me,' wait a day or two for a 21-year-old that doesn't know what Linear does to say 'hey what do you do, how much are you willing to pay?'"

**On Account Executives (AEs):**

> "The classic human doing the sales—most of the tools aren't there yet. I think 70% of their jobs will be safe by the end of next year, but I think it will decline to 40 or 50%."

**The bifurcation of sales talent:**

> "AI is replacing the jobs people don't wanna do today, and it is displacing the mid-pack and the mediocre. **The best humans will get superpowers from AI, but I'm not sure the rest will.**"

### [01:00:00 - 01:15:00] How to Become "Hyper-Employable"

**Lenny Rachitsky:** Someone's listening to this like "oh man, my job is in trouble." What should they focus on?

**Jason's advice:**

**Critical insight:** Agents don't work out of the box.

> "It takes time to train these agents—they don't work out of the box. But when you dial them in, when you take your **best person or your best script** and you train an agent with them, that agent can start to become a version of your best salesperson."

**Key Framework: The Orchestration Layer**

> "A dedicated person—in my case, my Chief AI Officer—must spend **1-2 hours daily reviewing agent outputs and managing the system.** These agents work 24/7, including weekends and holidays. They need oversight."

**The importance of vendor selection:**

Jason urges most companies to **purchase ready-made AI GTM tools** rather than build in-house:

> "The classic software buy-vs-build logic still applies. Unless you're Vercel—where they can build anything internally—most folks should buy."

## Key Frameworks & Mental Models

### 1. The "1.2 Humans + 20 Agents" Model
Replace scale-oriented roles with AI agents while keeping high-value human judgment. The 0.2 FTE is a dedicated "orchestrator" who reviews outputs and manages the system daily.

### 2. The Digital Best Salesperson Principle
Train AI agents exclusively on your **best salesperson's emails and scripts**. The agent becomes a scalable version of your top performer, not an average of your team.

### 3. The 30-Day Training Rule
AI agents require **30+ days of active training and correction** before they become effective. Vendors who help with this process are worth their premium.

### 4. The Orchestration Tax
Someone must spend **1-2 hours daily** reviewing agent outputs, correcting mistakes, and managing the system. This isn't set-it-and-forget-it.

### 5. The SDR Extinction Timeline
**Email-based SDRs will be 90% displaced within a year.** Inbound lead qualifiers (BDRs) should be "extinct next year." Human door-knockers and relationship builders remain valuable.

### 6. Hyper-Employability Formula
Pick one agentic tool, ingest your data, answer training questions daily for 30 days, deeply understand how it works. This skill set makes you "hyper-employable."

### 7. The Buy-vs-Build Decision Matrix
Unless you're a hyper-growing AI company with unlimited engineering resources (like Vercel), **buy don't build**. Classic software economics still apply.

### 8. The 50% In-Market Phenomenon
Traditional SaaS: 3-5% of prospects in-market per year. AI-native tools with massive ROI: **50%+ in market simultaneously.** This creates an unprecedented window for go-to-market efficiency.
