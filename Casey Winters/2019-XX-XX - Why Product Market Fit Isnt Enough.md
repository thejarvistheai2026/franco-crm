# Casey Winters - Why Product Market Fit Isn't Enough

**Date:** 2019  
**Source:** Reforge Essay

---

## About This Essay

Winters argues that product-market fit is necessary but insufficient for growth. Many products with PMF still struggle to scale because they haven't solved for distribution, retention, or other growth fundamentals.

---

## The Essay

### The PMF Myth

**Casey Winters:** Product-market fit is not enough. It's table stakes. The real question is: can you acquire users profitably, keep them engaged, and grow systematically?

People treat PMF like a finish line. It's not. It's the starting line.

### The Four Growth Pillars

**Winters:** Growth depends on four things:
1. **Acquisition:** Can you get users?
2. **Activation:** Can users experience value quickly?
3. **Retention:** Do users keep coming back?
4. **Monetization:** Can you make money?

PMF only addresses #1 and #2 indirectly. It doesn't guarantee #3 or #4.

### Why PMF Isn't Enough

**Winters:** I've seen products with clear PMF fail because:
- **Acquisition costs were too high** (even happy users are expensive to acquire)
- **Retention was poor** (people liked it but didn't need it regularly)
- **Market was too small** (clear PMF in a tiny market)
- **Couldn't monetize** (happy users won't pay)
- **Couldn't scale** (operational complexity)

### The Retention Problem

**Winters:** This is the biggest one. You can have users who love your product but only need it occasionally.

Think about productivity tools. People love them. But they use them once a week, not once a day. That limits growth potential.

### What You Actually Need

**Winters:** You need:
1. **Product-Market Fit** — people want this
2. **Product-Channel Fit** — can acquire users profitably
3. **Retention** — users come back repeatedly
4. **Network Effects** — ideally, the product gets better as it grows

### The Pinterest Example

**Winters:** Pinterest had clear PMF. Women loved it. But growth plateaued.

Why? We hadn't solved for:
- **Product-Channel Fit:** Ads were expensive, organic growth stalled
- **Adjacent Users:** Only serving one demographic
- **International:** US-centric, needed localization

We had PMF. We didn't have a growth engine.

### The Real Work

**Winters:** Post-PMF, the work changes. Instead of "what do users want?" it's:
- How do we reduce acquisition costs?
- How do we improve activation?
- How do we increase retention?
- How do we reactivate dormant users?
- How do we expand from this user type to the next?

This is growth work.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. PMF Is Table Stakes
It's necessary but not sufficient. The bar is higher.

### 2. Retention Is Underrated
The best products have high retention. Not just "people like it" but "people can't do without it."

### 3. You Need Product-Channel Fit
Can you acquire users efficiently? Not just "can you get users" but "can you get them profitably?"

### 4. Growth Is Systematic
Growth requires building systems: loops, processes, optimization, not just launching features.

### 5. The Work Changes
Pre-PMF: Discovery, finding fit. Post-PMF: Optimization, expansion, efficiency.

## Application

### For Early-Stage Founders
Don't assume PMF guarantees success. Plan for the growth phase before you need it.

### For Growth Teams
Build metrics around the four pillars. Don't just optimize acquisition.

### For Investors
Ask: "Beyond PMF, what evidence do you have of sustainable growth?"
