### Key Mental Models
- **Circle of Competence:** Know your edge; play games you can win
- **First Principles:** Break down to irreducible truths
- **Second-Order Thinking:** Ask "And then what?"
- **Inversion:** Avoid failure vs. seek success
- **Map is Not Territory:** Models ≠ reality
- **Margin of Safety:** Room for error
- **Probabilistic Thinking:** Navigate uncertainty
- **Chesterton's Fence:** Understand before changing

### Unique Angle
Shane popularized Charlie Munger's concept of a "latticework of mental models" — combining insights from multiple disciplines (physics, biology, economics, psychology) to make better decisions. His work bridges academic concepts with practical application, making mental models accessible for everyday decision-making.

### The Latticework Concept
A single mental model (like a hammer) leads to over-application. A latticework of many models allows for nuanced, multi-faceted thinking. Shane's contribution is organizing ~100 models by discipline and showing how they interconnect.
