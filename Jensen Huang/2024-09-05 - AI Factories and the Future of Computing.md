## Interview Transcript

### The AI Factory Concept

**Interviewer:** You talk about AI factories. What is an AI factory?

**Jensen Huang:** Let me explain the difference. The data center of the past processed data. It stored data. It retrieved data. It processed transactions. That's what traditional data centers do.

The data center of the future generates intelligence. It produces tokens. It reasons. It understands. It generates content, insights, decisions.

An AI factory is a facility that runs 24/7, generating intelligence for whoever uses it — whether that's a researcher, an enterprise, or consumers around the world.

Just like electricity was generated by power plants, intelligence is now generated by AI factories. That's why I call them AI factories.

### Generative AI as New Computing Paradigm

**Interviewer:** Why is generative AI different from previous AI waves?

**Jensen Huang:** Previous AI waves were about perception — recognizing images, understanding speech, detecting patterns.

Generative AI is about reasoning and creation. The computer is now reasoning. The computer is now generating. This is fundamental.

The transition from retrieval computing to generative computing is as big as the transition from mainframes to PCs, or from PCs to the internet, or from the internet to mobile.

Think about it: In the past, you used computers to retrieve information. You would type a query, and the computer would retrieve documents. That's retrieval computing, and it's what we've been doing for decades.

Now, you describe to the computer what you want, and the computer generates it. That's generative computing. This is a brand new way of computing.

### Why GPUs, Not CPUs

**Interviewer:** Why are GPUs the right architecture for AI?

**Jensen Huang:** Moore's Law is dead. You can't get more performance from a CPU by making transistors smaller anymore. The physics doesn't work.

But the demand for computing continues to grow exponentially. How do you solve this? You accelerate. You use specialized processors designed for specific workloads.

GPUs are ideal for AI because AI — especially deep learning — is embarrassingly parallel. The calculations can be done simultaneously across thousands of processors.

A CPU is like a race car — great for getting one thing done fast. A GPU is like a fleet of trucks — great for moving massive amounts of cargo at once.

AI workloads need the trucks, not the race car.

### The Three Computers for Physical AI

**Interviewer:** You've talked about three computers. Can you explain?

**Jensen Huang:** For generative AI in the data center, you have two computers: one for training and one for inference.

But for physical AI — robots, cars, factories — you actually have three computers.

First, you have the training computer. That's DGX. You train the AI in the data center.

Second, you have the runtime computer. That's in the robot or the car. It runs the AI in real-time.

Third — and this is crucial — you have the simulation computer. That's Omniverse. You use it to simulate physics, to test the AI in virtual worlds before deploying it in the real world.

The third computer is what enables physical AI to be safe and reliable. You can test millions of miles in simulation before driving a single real mile.

### Democratization of Intelligence

**Interviewer:** How does this democratize AI?

**Jensen Huang:** Think about it this way: 30 years ago, if you wanted to reach customers, you needed a store. You needed expensive real estate. It was hard.

Then the internet came along. And suddenly, everyone could have a website. Global reach for essentially free.

We're doing the same thing with AI. Every company will have their own AI. Every company will generate tokens. Every company will have intelligence as a service.

You don't need to be a tech giant. You don't need to hire thousands of AI researchers. You can use NVIDIA's AI foundry services, or deploy your own models using our software.

Democratizing access to AI is one of the most important things we're doing.

### The Scale of Transformation

**Interviewer:** How big is this transformation?

**Jensen Huang:** This is a $100 trillion industry transformation.

Think about every industry that exists today. Healthcare. Finance. Transportation. Manufacturing. Entertainment.

Every single one of them will be reinvented by AI.

Not augmented. Not assisted. Reinvented.

You'll have AI doctors. AI engineers. AI lawyers. AI scientists. AI advisors. AI tutors.

This isn't about replacing humans. It's about amplifying humans. It's about giving every person superpowers.

### Energy Efficiency and Sustainability

**Interviewer:** What about the energy requirements?

**Jensen Huang:** Here's what people miss: accelerated computing is the most sustainable form of computing.

If you tried to power AI using CPUs, the energy consumption would be unsustainable. You'd need power plants the size of cities.

But with GPUs, you get orders of magnitude better performance per watt. Blackwell — our next generation — delivers 25x better performance per watt for inference.

We're not just making computing faster. We're making it more efficient. We're making it sustainable.

The people building 100 megawatt, 500 megawatt, gigawatt data centers — they're doing it with accelerated computing because it's the only way to scale.

### The Future: 10 Years From Now

**Interviewer:** Where will we be in 10 years?

**Jensen Huang:** In 10 years, AI will be everywhere. Every application. Every device. Every service.

You'll have AI assistants that understand you completely. AI doctors that monitor your health continuously. AI scientists that run experiments 24/7.

The line between the digital and physical worlds will blur. With Omniverse, digital twins will run entire factories, entire cities before they're built.

Robots will be part of daily life — not just vacuum cleaners, but humanoid assistants, delivery robots, construction robots.

And it will seem normal. Just like the internet seemed radical in 1995 and normal in 2005, AI will seem normal by 2034.
