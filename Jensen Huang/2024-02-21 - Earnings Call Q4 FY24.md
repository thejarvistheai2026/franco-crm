## Earnings Call Transcript Highlights

### Opening Remarks

**Jensen Huang:** Good afternoon, everyone. Thank you for joining us today.

NVIDIA had an exceptional quarter and an exceptional year. For the fourth quarter, revenue was a record $22.1 billion, up 22% sequentially and up 265% from a year ago.

Our Data Center revenue reached $18.4 billion, up 27% sequentially and up 409% year on year.

Full-year revenue was $60.9 billion, up 126% from the prior year.

### The Tipping Point Has Been Reached

**Jensen Huang:** Accelerated computing and generative AI have hit the tipping point. Demand is surging worldwide across companies, industries and nations.

Our Data Center platform is powered by increasingly diverse drivers — demand for data processing, training and inference from large cloud-service providers and GPU-specialized ones, as well as from enterprise software and consumer internet companies.

Vertical industries — led by auto, financial services and healthcare — are now at a multibillion-dollar level.

### Cloud Partnerships

**Jensen Huang:** All major cloud providers are deploying our AI infrastructure.

We expanded our strategic collaboration with Amazon Web Services to host NVIDIA DGX Cloud on AWS.

We announced collaborations with Google to optimize NVIDIA's data center and PC AI platforms for Gemma, Google's groundbreaking open language models.

Our H100 GPU is deployed across every major cloud. We're working with Microsoft Azure, Google Cloud Platform, AWS, and Oracle Cloud.

### Enterprise Adoption

**Jensen Huang:** Enterprise adoption is accelerating. We announced that Amgen will use the NVIDIA DGX SuperPOD to power insights into drug discovery, diagnostics and precision medicine.

We introduced NVIDIA NeMo Retriever, a generative AI microservice that lets enterprises connect custom large language models with enterprise data.

Singtel will bring generative AI services to Singapore through energy-efficient data centers built with NVIDIA Hopper architecture GPUs.

And we announced plans with Cisco to help enterprises quickly and easily deploy and manage secure AI infrastructure.

### Consumer AI: RTX Platform

**Jensen Huang:** NVIDIA RTX, introduced less than six years ago, is now a massive PC platform for generative AI, enjoyed by 100 million gamers and creators.

We launched GeForce RTX 40 SUPER Series GPUs, starting at $599, supporting the latest NVIDIA RTX technologies including DLSS 3.5 Ray Reconstruction and NVIDIA Reflex.

We announced generative AI capabilities for our installed base of over 100 million RTX AI PCs, including TensorRT-LLM to accelerate inference on large language models, and Chat with RTX, a tech demo that lets users personalize a chatbot with their own content.

### Gaming and Professional Visualization

**Jensen Huang:** Gaming revenue of $2.9 billion was up 56% year on year. Full-year gaming revenue rose 15% to $10.4 billion.

Professional Visualization revenue of $463 million was up 105% from a year ago.

Automotive revenue of $281 million was up 8% sequentially. Full-year automotive revenue rose 21% to $1.1 billion.

### Looking Ahead: GTC 2024

**Jensen Huang:** The year ahead will bring major new product cycles with exceptional innovations to help propel our industry forward.

Come join us at next month's GTC, where we and our rich ecosystem will reveal the exciting future ahead.

We expect continued strong demand for our data center products as enterprises and cloud providers race to build AI infrastructure.

### Closing

**Jensen Huang:** For 31 years, NVIDIA has been at the forefront of accelerated computing. This technology has now become the engine of AI.

We're grateful to our partners and customers who trust us with their most important AI initiatives.

Thank you for your support.
