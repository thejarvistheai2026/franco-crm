## Full Transcript - The Denny's Story

### Background: From Taiwan to America

Jensen Huang was born in Taipei, Taiwan in 1963. At age 9, he and his brother were sent to the United States to live with an uncle in Tacoma, Washington, escaping social unrest in Thailand where their father worked. Due to a misunderstanding, they were enrolled at the Oneida Baptist Institute in Kentucky—a religious reform academy for troubled youth—instead of the prestigious boarding school their parents intended.

At the academy, Jensen cleaned toilets daily, learned to play table tennis, appeared in Sports Illustrated at age 14, and taught an illiterate 17-year-old roommate to read in exchange for learning how to bench press.

### The Denny's Job

Beginning at age 15, Huang worked graveyard shifts at a local Denny's restaurant in Oregon from 1978 to 1983 as a dishwasher, busboy, and waiter. He recalled: "I was the youngest kid in school, in class, and the only student who looked like a child."

Years later, this experience would prove pivotal when founding Nvidia.

### The Founding Meeting

After working at AMD and LSI Logic in Silicon Valley, Huang met Chris Malachowsky and Curtis Priem at Sun Microsystems where they were developing graphics accelerators. In 1992, as the computer industry slowed, the three decided to leave their jobs to create a company making graphics chips for PC games.

The three met frequently in 1992 at a Denny's roadside diner in East San Jose to formulate a business plan. Huang chose Denny's because of his prior work experience there, and because it was "quieter than home and had cheap coffee."

They sat in a breakfast booth, sketching out the company's future. The name "Nvidia" was suggested by Priem based on the Latin word "invidia." They dropped the "i" to honor the NV1 chip they were developing.

### Initial Capital and Incorporation

To incorporate the company, lawyer James Gaither demanded the $200 in cash Huang had in his pockets to capitalize the company. Huang then asked each co-founder for $200—making Nvidia's initial capital a total of $600.

On April 5, 1993, Huang personally signed Nvidia's original articles of incorporation.

### Early Struggles and Near-Bankruptcy

Nvidia's first product, the NV1, was a commercial failure. The company chose quadratic surfaces instead of triangles for 3D graphics—a bet that turned out to be wrong. The company was burning through cash fast.

In one famous story, Huang had to fly to give a presentation to Sega. The meeting was going badly until Sega's executives asked what he would do if they invested. Huang replied: "If you invest in us, I'll give it everything I've got." This honesty won them over.

The company pivoted to triangle-based rendering with the RIVA 128 in 1997, which became their first hit.

## Key Themes

### 1. Persistence Through Failure
- First product completely wrong architecture
- Near-bankruptcy multiple times in the 1990s
- "The darkest days make the brightest nights"

### 2. Founding on First Principles
- Three engineers believed in graphics computing
- $600 initial capital
- Persistence despite every reason to quit

### 3. Long-Term Thinking
- Still CEO 30+ years after founding
- "I'm doing exactly what I wanted to do my whole life"
- Focus on the future, not quarterly results

### 4. The "Denny's Philosophy"
- "No task is beneath me"
- Willingness to do any job
- Stay connected to ground-level reality
