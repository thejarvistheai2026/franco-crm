# Elon Musk - TED 2017: The Future We're Building

**Date:** April 28, 2017
**Source:** TED Conference, Vancouver
**Location:** Vancouver, Canada
**Interviewer:** Chris Anderson
**Guest:** Elon Musk (CEO of Tesla, SpaceX)

---

## About This Interview

Elon Musk delivered a surprise appearance at TED 2017 with an on-stage interview with TED curator Chris Anderson. The conversation covered the Boring Company (revealing the first tunnel concept), Tesla's future, SpaceX's Mars plans, and the first detailed look at the Model 3.

This interview was historic for:
- First Boring Company public demonstration (tunnel video)
- First Model 3 showing on stage
- Detailed technical discussion of Mars mission economics
- Showcasing Musk's vision for integrated sustainable transport

---

## The Boring Company

### The "Why"

**Chris Anderson:** You started a tunneling company. Why?

**Elon Musk:** Traffic is driving me nuts. I'm going to build a tunnel boring machine and just start digging.

**Anderson:** That's how it started?

**Musk:** Literally. I was stuck in traffic. I tweeted "I'm going to build a tunnel boring machine." Two hours later, I bought a domain name. TheBoringCompany.com.

**Anderson:** But how does this scale? One tunnel?

**Musk:** The key insight is that the ground is 3D. We have roads in 2D, which creates traffic. But you can go down many levels. The ground is practically unlimited.

### Cost Reduction

**Anderson:** Tunneling is expensive. How do you make it cheaper?

**Musk:** Current tunneling costs about $1 billion per mile. We think we can reduce that by at least a factor of 10.

**Anderson:** How?

**Musk:** Several things. First, smaller tunnels. Current tunnels are built for the largest possible vehicle. We only need tunnels for cars. That reduces diameter by half, which reduces cost by a factor of 4.

**Anderson:** Just from diameter?

**Musk:** Yes. Tunnels scale with the square of diameter. Half diameter means quarter cost.

**Anderson:** What else?

**Musk:** Speed. Current boring machines are very slow. We're designing machines that go much faster. And we're doing the dirt removal more efficiently.

### The Vision

**Anderson:** Show us what this looks like.

**Musk:** [Video plays showing cars on skates traveling through tunnels at high speed]

**Anderson:** So cars go on these electric skates?

**Musk:** Yes. You drive onto a platform at street level. The car locks in place. You're lowered into the tunnel. Then you travel at 120 miles per hour to your destination.

**Anderson:** And emissions?

**Musk:** The skates are electric. The tunnels have ventilation and air scrubbing. It's actually much better for the environment than surface roads.

## Tesla and Model 3

### The Model 3 Reveal

**Anderson:** You've got something to show us?

**Musk:** Yeah, first public showing of the Model 3. [Model 3 drives onto stage]

**Anderson:** This is the $35,000 Tesla?

**Musk:** Base price $35,000. But most people will want some options. This one has all the options—it's about $55,000.

**Anderson:** You have 400,000 pre-orders.

**Musk:** More like 500,000 now. It's been overwhelming.

**Anderson:** Can you actually build them?

**Musk:** We're going to try. Next month we start production. We'll ramp over the year. The goal is 5,000 per week by end of year, 10,000 per week next year.

### The Economics of Scale

**Anderson:** Why is the Model 3 so important?

**Musk:** This is the car that makes sustainable energy mass market. The Model S and X prove we can do it. Model 3 proves we can do it for everyone.

**Anderson:** But 5,000 a week is 250,000 a year. That's small compared to GM or Toyota.

**Musk:** It's the start. Next factory will do 500,000. The one after that, a million. We're scaling exponentially.

**Anderson:** You're competing with companies that make 10 million vehicles.

**Musk:** They do, but they're making combustion engines. We're making electric. Different game.

## SpaceX and Mars

### The Mars Economics

**Anderson:** Let's talk Mars. You said $200,000 per person. How?

**Musk:** Four things. Full reusability. Refilling in orbit. Propellant production on Mars. Right propellant.

**Anderson:** Start with reusability.

**Musk:** Right now rockets are thrown away after one use. Imagine if aircraft were single-use. A 747 costs $300 million. If you threw it away every trip, ticket prices would be astronomical.

**Anderson:** But you've demonstrated landing.

**Musk:** Yeah, we've got 15 successful landings now. We're getting close to routine reusability.

### Refilling

**Anderson:** Refilling in orbit?

**Musk:** The ship goes to orbit with passengers and cargo. It meets up with a tanker in orbit. The tanker fills it up with propellant. Then it goes to Mars.

**Anderson:** How much can you carry?

**Musk:** Without refilling, maybe 50 tons. With refilling, 200+ tons. That's the difference between a research base and a full colony.

**Anderson:** And the propellant on Mars?

**Musk:** Methane and oxygen from the Martian atmosphere. CO2 plus water gives you CH4 and O2. That's the return trip fuel.

### The Timeline

**Anderson:** When do you think humans go?

**Muss:** First cargo missions 2022. First crew 2024.

**Anderson:** That's seven years from now.

**Musk:** Yeah. We need to get there.

## The Billionaire Question

### Why Not Just Enjoy the Money?

**Anderson:** You're a billionaire. Why not just enjoy life?

**Musk:** (pause) I think it's important for there to be an extension of life beyond Earth. I think this is important for the future of humanity.

**Anderson:** But why you? Why do you have to do it?

**Musk:** I don't have to do it. But if I don't, who will?

**Anderson:** But you've got enough money to retire 100 times.

**Musk:** (laughs) Yeah, I could buy a private island and drink mai tais. But that's not interesting. There's too much to do.

### The Meaning

**Anderson:** What motivates you?

**Musk:** I want to be able to think about the future and feel good about it. I want to wake up in the morning and think the future is going to be great.

**Anderson:** And Tesla, SpaceX—that's what makes you feel good?

**Musk:** These are things that are inspiring. That's what makes life worth living. The future is going to be great.

## Key Insights from TED

### 1. Tunnel Cost Scaling
Cut diameter in half → cost drops by factor of 4. Diameter 2nd power relationship is tunnel economics key.

### 2. 3D vs 2D Transport
Ground is 3D. Surface roads are 2D. Traffic is scarcity of 2D space. Unlimited 3D solves it.

### 3. Model 3 = Mass Market EV
S/X proved possible. Model 3 proves scalable (35K price point = accessibility).

### 4. Refilling Multiplies Payload
50 tons → 200+ tons with orbital refilling. That's research base vs. colony difference.

### 5. Reusability First Principles
Aircraft analogy: Single-use 747 = $1M tickets. Reusability enables affordable travel.

### 6. Why You = Why Not Someone Else
"I don't have to do it. But if I don't, who will?" No one else was doing it, so he did.

### 7. Inspiration over Retirement
"Could buy private island. But that's not interesting." Work itself is the reward.

## Context and Impact

TED 2017 was pivotal for Musk's public profile:
- **Boring Company**: Went from joke to serious company (video demo validated it)
- **Model 3**: First public showing built massive anticipation
- **Mars Timeline**: 2024 human mission target became public commitment
- **Reusability**: 15 successful landings proved the concept worked

**Impact:**
- Boring Company raised $113M from private investors
- Model 3 went into production the next month (July 2017)
- Mars conversation intensified public interest
- The "why you?" exchange became defining Musk philosophy moment

The Model 3 showing was brief—car drove on stage, Musk got out, that was it. But it was enough. The world wanted this car.
