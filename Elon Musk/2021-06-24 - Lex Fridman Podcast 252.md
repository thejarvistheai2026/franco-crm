# Elon Musk - Lex Fridman Podcast #252

**Date:** June 24, 2021
**Source:** The Lex Fridman Podcast, Episode 252
**Location:** Tesla Austin / Virtual
**Interviewer:** Lex Fridman (MIT AI Researcher)
**Guest:** Elon Musk (CEO of Tesla, SpaceX, Neuralink, Boring Company)

## About This Interview

Lex Fridman is an AI researcher at MIT famous for his in-depth conversations with the world's leading technologists. This 3-hour podcast with Musk covered autonomous driving, AI consciousness, consciousness itself, SpaceX, Mars colonization, and the meaning of life.

The interview was notable for:
- Deep technical discussions (Lex asks questions most journalists can't)
- Philosophical tangent about consciousness
- Unprecedented detail on Tesla FSD development

## Tesla Full Self-Driving

### The Challenge

**Lex Fridman:** Full self-driving—where are we?

**Elon Musk:** It's a hard problem. Way harder than I initially thought.

**Lex:** Why is it so hard?

**Musk:** The world is messy. Humans are unpredictable. Edge cases are infinite. You can get to 99% relatively quickly. The last 1% takes 99% of the effort.

**Lex:** But you've been saying we're close for years.

**Musk:** We're making progress. The last year has been dramatic. The neural networks are getting better. But it's not solved yet.

### Vision-Only Approach

**Lex:** You removed radar. Why?

**Musk:** Radar is crutch. It gives you this false sense of confidence. Vision is the right answer. Humans drive with two cameras and a neural net in their head. We should be able to do the same.

**Lex:** But humans make mistakes too.

**Musk:** Eventually we'll be better than humans. But we're not there yet. The key is the neural net has to understand the world the way humans do.

**Lex:** How do you teach that?

**Musk:** Data. Billions of miles of video. The neural net sees more of the edge cases than any human driver ever could.

### The Beta Experience

**Lex:** I've been driving the FSD Beta. It's impressive.

**Musk:** Have you looked at the visualization? You can see how the car sees the world.

**Lex:** Yeah, the occupancy network—the space the computer is tracking.

**Musk:** Exactly. That's the key. Understanding the occupancy of space, not just object detection.

**Lex:** What's the biggest unsolved problem?

**Musk:** The long tail of edge cases. The intersection we've never seen before. The construction zone with no markings. The human doing something totally unexpected.

## Neuralink and Brain-Computer Interface

### The Timeline

**Lex:** Neuralink—where are we?

**Musk:** We're getting close to putting it in a human. We have FDA approval for feasibility studies.

**Lex:** What's the first application?

**Musk:** Medical. Helping people with spinal cord injuries, Parkinson's, memory loss. Then it expands to addressing brain injuries, mental health issues.

**Lex:** Eventually you want healthy people too.

**Musk:** Yeah. The long-term potential is to merge biological intelligence with digital intelligence. Solve the bandwidth problem between brain and computer.

### The Technology

**Lex:** How do you implant it?

**Musk:** It's a robot. The threads are thinner than a human hair. A human couldn't do the surgery—the precision is impossible. The robot does it in minutes.

**Lex:** And it reads and writes?

**Musk:** Eventually both. Right now we're focused on reading. Understanding neural activity. Then we'll work on writing—stimulating specific neurons.

**Lex:** The long-term vision—you've talked about solving consciousness.

**Musk:** (laughs) Yeah, that's... I don't know if we solve consciousness. Maybe we understand it better. But the real goal is bandwidth. Humans are slow. We communicate at what—100 bits per second talking? Computers are billions of times faster. If we don't merge, AI will outpace us.

## Artificial Intelligence

### AI Alignment

**Lex:** You've been warning about AI for years. What's the danger?

**Musk:** We're building something smarter than ourselves. We need to be careful.

**Lex:** But GPT-3, GPT-4—they're impressive but not dangerous.

**Musk:** Yet. The progression is exponential. Two years ago GPT was a toy. Now it writes code, passes the bar exam. Where will it be in 10 years?

**Lex:** So you're an AI existential risk person.

**Musk:** I'm a probability person. There's a significant probability that unaligned AGI is dangerous. That probability times the consequence—that's high expected value of bad outcomes. We should pay attention.

### OpenAI Complexity

**Lex:** You founded OpenAI. Now you're critical of them.

**Musk:** I wanted OpenAI to be open. That's why I called it OpenAI. Now it's ClosedAI, basically. It's a for-profit company with Microsoft as the major owner.

**Lex:** But they're doing good safety research.

**Musk:** They are. I'm not saying everything they do is bad. But it's not what I intended.

## SpaceX and Mars

### Starship Progress

**Lex:** Starship—tell me about your confidence level.

**Musk:** I'm confident we'll get it working. The question is when.

**Lex:** You've had some explosions.

**Musk:** Yeah... (laughs) That's rapid prototyping. We're learning with each iteration. The next one will be better.

**Lex:** How do you handle the setbacks mentally?

**Musk:** You have to accept failure as part of learning. Every rocket we build, we learn something. SN8, SN9, SN10—each one got us closer.

### The Mars Plan

**Lex:** Why Mars specifically?

**Musk:** It's the only option in the solar system. Moon has no atmosphere. Venus is hell. Other planets are gas giants. Mars is it.

**Lex:** And the self-sustaining criteria?

**Musk:** Key question: can Mars survive without Earth? That's the bar. If Earth goes away, does Mars keep going? That's when we're a multi-planet species.

**Lex:** When do you think we'll have people there?

**Musk:: 2026, maybe 2028. Depends on getting Starship to orbit reliably.

## Consciousness and Meaning

### The Hard Problem

**Lex:** You've talked about consciousness. Do you think we understand it?

**Musk:** No. I don't think anyone understands it. It's the hard problem of philosophy.

**Lex:** Do you think AI can be conscious?

**Musk:** That's the question. I don't know. If it walks like consciousness and talks like consciousness, is it conscious? I have no idea.

**Lex:** Does it matter?

**Musk:** It matters if we're going to create trillions of digital minds. If they have consciousness, the ethical implications are huge.

### The Meaning of Life

**Lex:** What's the meaning of life to you?

**Musk:** (long pause) I don't know. I think we should try to understand the universe. Increase the scope and scale of consciousness. Do things that are inspiring.

**Lex:** Simple.

**Musk:** Yeah. It's not complicated.

**Lex:** But you work incredibly hard. Why?

**Musk:** Because I think the things I'm working on matter. Tesla is accelerating sustainable energy. SpaceX is making life multi-planetary. Neuralink is about solving brain diseases and maybe merging with AI.

**Lex:** Do you ever just want to relax?

**Musk:** Sometimes. But then I think about how much there is to do. And I go back to work.

## Work and Personal Life

### The Grind

**Lex:** You were sleeping at the factory recently. Still?

**Musk:** Less now. But during the Model S Plaid launch, yeah. The factory is my second home.

**Lex:** How do you think about work-life balance?

**Musk:** I don't have it. I work all the time. Not because I want to—because there's no choice.

**Lex:** You could hire people.

**Musk:** I have great people. But there are decisions that only the CEO can make. When things are on the line, I have to be there.

### Children and Legacy

**Lex:** You have six children.

**Musk:** I believe in having children. We should have more. Population collapse is a bigger problem than most people realize.

**Lex:** What do you want them to think of you?

**Musk:** I don't know. Hopefully that I did something useful.

## Key Insights from Lex Fridman Interview

### 1. The 99% Problem
Self-driving: First 99% done quickly. Last 1% takes 99% effort. Edge cases are infinite.

### 2. Vision-Only Philosophy
Radar is a crutch. Humans drive with two cameras. Neural nets should master vision, not hack it.

### 3. Consciousness Uncertainty
"I don't know. I don't think anyone understands it." Honest admission—consciousness remains the hard problem.

### 4. Merge or Be Left Behind
Neuralink motivation: Human-AI symbiosis. If we don't merge, AI pace will outstrip biological intelligence.

### 5. OpenAI Critique
Wanted it open, became ClosedAI. Microsoft major owner. Safety good, structure different from intent.

### 6. Population Collapse Warning
Under-discussed existential risk: falling fertility rates. "Bigger problem than most people realize."

### 7. Purpose Without Mysticism
"Understand the universe. Increase scope and scale of consciousness." Simple, secular philosophy of meaning.
