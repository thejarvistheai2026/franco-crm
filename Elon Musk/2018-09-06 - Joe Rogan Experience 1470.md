# Elon Musk - Joe Rogan Experience #1470

**Date:** September 6, 2018  
**Source:** The Joe Rogan Experience, Episode 1470  
**Location:** Los Angeles, California  
**Interviewer:** Joe Rogan  
**Guest:** Elon Musk (CEO of Tesla, SpaceX, Neuralink, Boring Company)

---

## About This Interview

This two-and-a-half-hour interview became one of the most influential and memed podcast episodes in history. Elon Musk smoked cannabis with Joe Rogan, discussed AI risks, aliens, flamethrowers, and gave unprecedented insight into his mindset.

Within 48 hours of this interview:
- Tesla stock dropped 9%
- The Air Force reportedly reviewed Musk's security clearance
- "Funding secured" SEC investigation intensified
- "Smoking Elon" became the meme of the week

This conversation revealed Musk at his most open and unfiltered.

## A.I. Dangers

### The Simulation Argument

**Rogan:** A lot of people think we're living in a simulation.

**Musk:** I've had so many simulation discussions it's crazy. In fact, it got to the point where basically every conversation was about the simulation. My brother and I would go, "Are we in a base reality?" It would drive us nuts.

**Rogan:** So what do you think? Are we?

**Musk:** Probably not, but it's one of the possibilities. The argument for the simulation is quite strong in my view.

**Rogan:** Explain it to people who don't know.

**Musk:** Forty years ago we had Pong. Two rectangles and a dot. That's what games were. Now forty years later, we have photorealistic 3D simulations with millions of people playing simultaneously. It's getting better every year.

So if you assume any rate of improvement at all, then games will become indistinguishable from reality. Even if that rate of advancement drops by a thousand from what it is right now, let's just imagine it's 0.1% as effective... then you'd still have that indistinguishability ten thousand years before we're supposed to be extinct.

**Rogan:** Right. So basically at some point there'll be simulations that are so realistic that the people in them will think they're real.

**Musk:** And then if you have civilization that can create simulations, and they create billions of simulations, then what are the odds that we're in base reality?

**Rogan:** Almost none.

**Musk:** Yeah, it's one in billions.

### AI as an Existential Threat

**Rogan:** You've talked about AI being dangerous.

**Musk:** AI is far more dangerous than nukes. So why is there no regulatory oversight? This is insane.

**Rogan:** You really think it's that dangerous?

**Musk:** With AI, we are summoning the demon. In all those stories where the guy with the pentagram and holy water is like, yeah, he's sure he can control the demon. Didn't work out.

**Rogan:** What makes you think that?

**Musk:** The pace of progress in AI is really incredible. I've been warning about AI for years. The danger of AI is much greater than the danger of nuclear warheads by a lot. And nobody would suggest that we allow the world to just build nuclear warheads if they want. That's insane.

**Rogan:** Why do you think it's so dangerous specifically?

**Musk:** The danger is going to be in the software. There's going to be an equivalent of a cyber war with AI. And it's going to get out of control. If there's something that can think and learn at a billion times faster rate than a human, what's the point of humans?

**Rogan:** You think it will view us as we're not necessary anymore?

**Musk:** I hope the computers decide we're like house cats. But I could be wrong.

## Innovation and Companies

### The Boring Company

**Rogan:** Why did you start the Boring Company?

**Musk:** I'm a big proponent of tunnels. You can have tunnels that are many levels deep. The ground is basically 3D. The reason we have traffic is because we only have 2D transportation. But you can go down many levels.

**Rogan:** How did it start?

**Musk:** It started as a joke. I started digging a tunnel under my desk at SpaceX. Literally just with a regular boring machine. Then I was like, you know what, this could actually work.

**Rogan:** And the flamethrower was a joke too?

**Musk:** Yeah, that was just a joke. I was like, we're going to sell a flamethrower. And I thought like maybe 5 people would buy it. We sold 20,000 of them.

**Rogan:** (laughs) Why do you think people bought them?

**Musk:** It's fun to have a flamethrower. You can like, light your barbecue really fast.

### Neuralink

**Rogan:** Tell me about Neuralink.

**Musk:** The initial goal is to solve important brain and spine problems. There are a lot of them—memory loss, hearing loss, blindness, paralysis, depression, insomnia, extreme pain, seizures, anxiety. All these things that people experience that have some kind of fundamental issue with the computer that is the brain.

**Rogan:** So you're trying to reprogram it?

**Musk:** You're trying to fix whatever went wrong. Then there's the far longer-term aspiration, which is to achieve a symbiosis with artificial intelligence. If you can't beat them, join them.

**Rogan:** So you're talking about merging with AI?

**Musk:** Yeah. The thing that's going to be dangerous is a digital intelligence that is vastly smarter than humans. If you're part of that, if your biological intelligence can merge with the digital intelligence that's symbiotic, then you're part of it.

**Rogan:** You'll have some say in what it does.

**Musk:** Exactly. The purpose of Neuralink is to create a high-bandwidth interface to the brain.

## SpaceX and Mars

### The Starship Vision

**Rogan:** Where do we stand with going to Mars?

**Musk:** We're getting close. We're building the ship right now. It's called Starship. It used to be called the Mars Colonial Transporter. People thought that was too ambitious, so we changed it to Interplanetary Transport System. Still too ambitious, so we call it Starship.

**Rogan:** When do you think we'll go?

**Musk:** The first cargo missions will probably go in 2022. The first crewed missions, 2024-2026.

**Rogan:** That's pretty soon.

**Musk:** I think we'll be able to do it. The rocket will be able to do it. The question is whether we can solve the technology to keep people alive.

**Rogan:** How do you solve that?

**Musk:** Radiation is a problem. But the bigger problem is just making sure you have enough supplies. Once you get there, you have to set up a propellant plant. You need to make methane and oxygen from the Martian atmosphere.

### Why Mars?

**Rogan:** Why do you think we need to go to Mars?

**Musk:** We're going to face an inevitable extinction event. There have been multiple extinction events in the past, and there will be more in the future. The probability of some event happening to Earth is 100%. It could be something that we make ourselves, like nuclear war or runaway AI, or something natural like an asteroid or volcano.

**Rogan:** So Mars is the escape hatch?

**Musk:** Mars is the backup drive for civilization. If we can make Mars self-sustaining, then humanity continues even if something catastrophic happens on Earth.

## Tesla and Work

### Tesla's Challenges

**Rogan:** You've been through hell with Tesla this year. Production hell.

**Musk:** It's been really intense. Yeah. We were trying to get the Model 3 production up, and it was just incredibly difficult. We were having to rewrite all the software for the production line.

**Rogan:** Why was it so hard?

**Musk:** Manufacturing is hard. We went from manufacturing a few thousand cars a year to trying to manufacture hundreds of thousands. The difficulty scales exponentially.

**Rogan:** You're sleeping at the factory.

**Musk:** Yeah, I was sleeping on the roof of the factory. In a sleeping bag. Just to be close to the production line.

**Rogan:** Why?

**Musk:** Because we had to get it done. The company was going to die if we didn't solve this. I had to be there.

### Work-Life Balance

**Rogan:** Do you ever think about slowing down?

**Musk:** I would if I could. But there's just so much to do.

**Rogan:** Don't you have advisors who tell you to chill?

**Musk:** They do. But the issue is that my hurts my brain and my heart. But I have to do it. I don't have a choice.

**Rogan:** You feel like you have to?

**Musk:** If I didn't do these things, then... Tesla would die. SpaceX might not die, but it would progress much slower. These things are important. The future of humanity depends on sustainable energy and being a multi-planetary species. I'm not doing this because I want to. I'm doing it because I have to.

## Key Insights from Joe Rogan

### 1. The Simulation Hypothesis
Most likely we're in a simulation: If any species creates simulations, they create billions of them. Odds of being in base reality: 1 in billions.

### 2. AI Risk Framing
"Summoning the demon" vs. "house cats for AI." The choice is: become AI's pets or merge with it.

### 3. The "Why" of Mars
Not exploration—extinction insurance. "Mars is the backup drive for civilization."

### 4. Manufacturing Scale
Difficulty scales exponentially not linearly: 10x production ≠ 10x difficulty. It's orders of magnitude harder.

### 5. Compulsion Over Ambition
"I'm not doing this because I want to. I'm doing it because I have to." The work chooses him, not vice versa.

### 6. Neuralink Purpose
Tier 1: Fix brain diseases. Tier 2: Merge with AI before it outpaces us.

### 7. Sleep Schedule
"Sleeping on factory roof in a sleeping bag"—literal description of his work ethic during Model 3 ramp.
