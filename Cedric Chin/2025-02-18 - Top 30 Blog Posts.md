## Overview
Cedric Chin writes Commoncog, a publication about accelerating business expertise. Formerly a software engineer who built a business from $0 to $4.5M ARR, Cedric focuses on the "dirty secret" of expertise: that most valuable business knowledge is tacit (can't be taught through words alone). His work spans career strategy, operator knowledge, Asian business conglomerates, and practical frameworks for navigating uncertain business environments.

**Core Philosophy:** Practice as the bar for truth. Most business skills are acquired through doing, not reading.

### 1. Career Moats 101
**Published:** April 16, 2023  
**Source:** commoncog.com/career-moats-101/  
**One-liner:** The comprehensive primer on building career moats — rare and valuable skills that protect your employability in a world of constant change.

### 3. If You Want a Great Career, Go After Rare & Valuable Skills
**Published:** 2018-2019  
**Source:** commoncog.com/if-you-want-a-great-career-go-after-rare-valuable-skills/

**Key Insights:**

### 19. Reduce Noise, Not Cognitive Biases
**Published:** 2020  
**Source:** commoncog.com/reduce-noise-not-cognitive-biases/  
**One-liner:** Behavioral economics overemphasizes bias correction; reducing decision noise often matters more.  
**Key Insights:**
- Noise = variability in decisions that should be identical
- Bias = systematic deviation from target
- In many domains, noise explains more error than bias
- Noise reduction methods: structured decision processes, checklists, second opinions
- Kahneman's "Noise" book argument
