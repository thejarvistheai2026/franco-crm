# Jeff Bezos - Amazon IPO Roadshow (1997)

**Date:** April 24, 1997  
**Source:** Amazon IPO Roadshow Presentations  
**Location:** Various investor meetings

---

## About This Presentation

Before Amazon went public in May 1997, Bezos presented his vision to institutional investors. This was his first major public articulation of the Amazon philosophy — one that would prove remarkably consistent over the next 25+ years.

---

## The Presentation

### Opening

**Jeff Bezos:** Amazon.com is the leading online retailer of books. We opened our virtual doors in July 1995 with a mission: to use the Internet to transform book buying into the fastest, easiest, and most enjoyable shopping experience possible.

### The Opportunity

**Bezos:** The Internet is growing at 2,300% per year. We have a first-mover advantage in what will become a massive market. But here's what investors need to understand: we're not optimizing for short-term profits. We're optimizing for long-term market leadership.

### Customer Obsession

**Bezos:** Our investors ask: "When will you be profitable?" We answer: "We don't know, and we don't care in the short term." What we care about is market share, customer satisfaction, and brand. These are the inputs that create long-term value.

### Competing with Barnes & Noble

**Bezos:** Barnes & Noble has 1,000 stores. We have one website. They have $2 billion in physical infrastructure. We have software. Their cost structure is fixed. Ours is variable. They can only serve customers near their stores. We can serve anyone, anywhere.

### The Long Term View

**Bezos:** We will make decisions that sacrifice short-term earnings for long-term market leadership. We will invest aggressively in customer acquisition, technology, and distribution. We believe this is the right approach for building lasting value.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Customer Obsession Over Competitor Focus
Even in 1997, Bezos emphasized focusing on customers rather than competitors.

### 2. Long-Term Thinking
The famous "long-term" philosophy was present from day one.

### 3. Regret Minimization Framework
Bezos often cited his "regret minimization framework" — project yourself to age 80 and minimize the number of regrets.

### 4. The Internet as Platform
Saw the Internet as fundamentally different from physical retail, not just a channel.

---

## Quotes

- "We're not optimizing for short-term profits. We're optimizing for long-term market leadership."
- "It's all about the long term."
- "Market leadership can translate directly to higher revenue, higher profitability, greater capital velocity, and correspondingly stronger returns."
