# Jeff Bezos - Fire Phone Interview with Walt Mossberg and Kara Swisher (2014)

**Date:** June 18, 2014  
**Source:** Recode/Code Conference  
**Interviewers:** Walt Mossberg and Kara Swisher  
**Location:** Rancho Palos Verdes, California

---

## About This Interview

Bezos introduced the Amazon Fire Phone, which would become a famous failure. This interview showcases his willingness to take big risks and his philosophy on failure.

---

## The Interview

### The Fire Phone Reveal

**Walt Mossberg:** Jeff, what is this?

**Jeff Bezos:** *[Holds up Fire Phone]* This is the Amazon Fire Phone. We didn't just build another phone. We built something different.

**Kara Swisher:** What's different about it?

**Bezos:** Dynamic Perspective. Firefly. MayDay. These are things you can't get on other phones.

### Dynamic Perspective

**Mossberg:** Explain Dynamic Perspective.

**Bezos:** It uses four corner cameras to track your face. The screen responds to how you look at it. It's 3D without glasses. But it's not gimmicky — it's useful.

### Firefly

**Swisher:** What's Firefly?

**Bezos:** Point your phone at anything — a book, a product, a TV show, a phone number — and we recognize it. Instantly. Then we help you do something with it. Buy it, get info, save it.

### On Failure

**Mossberg:** You've had failures before. The Fire is a bold move. Aren't you worried?

**Bezos:** If you're not willing to fail, you can't invent. We have a long history of building things that people love. And sometimes, things that don't work. That's okay.

**Swisher:** What's your failure philosophy?

**Bezos:** We want to fail at things that, if they worked, would matter. Fire Phone matters. If customers love it, it's a big deal.

### Customer Focus

**Mossberg:** Why build a phone at all?

**Bezos:** To serve customers better. The phone is the most important device in people's lives. Being in that room matters.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Willingness to Fail Publicly
Fire Phone was a risky bet that ultimately failed, but Bezos positioned it as part of the innovation process.

### 2. Differentiation Matters
Emphasized features that made Fire Phone unique, not just "another phone."

### 3. Customer Access
Positioned the phone as about being present where customers live.

### 4. Failure Philosophy
"Fail at things that, if they worked, would matter."

---

## Aftermath

The Fire Phone launched to poor reviews and weak sales. Amazon took a $170 million write-down and eventually discontinued it. However, the technology (Firefly, MayDay) informed future Amazon products like Alexa and Echo.
