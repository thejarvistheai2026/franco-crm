# Jeff Bezos - TED Talk: The Electricity of the Internet (2003)

**Date:** February 2003  
**Source:** TED Conference  
**Location:** Monterey, California

---

## About This Talk

Bezos used the analogy of electricity to explain why the Internet was still in its early days. This talk became one of his most cited speeches about the long-term nature of technological adoption.

---

## The Talk

### The Golden Age

**Jeff Bezos:** I want to tell you about the golden age of electricity. It lasted about 15 years, from roughly 1890 to 1905. During this time, we went from having no electricity to having widespread electric power. It was an incredibly transformative period.

### Comparing Electricity to the Internet

**Bezos:** Now, imagine it's 1900. I tell you I have a new technology that will change everything. It will improve productivity, transform industries, change how people live. But here's the catch: it'll take 30-40 years to really pay off.

Would you invest? Most people wouldn't. They'd want immediate returns.

### The Internet Today

**Bezos:** That's where we are with the Internet. We're in the early days. The skeptics look at the dot-com crash and say, "See? It was all hype." But they're missing the forest for the trees.

The Internet is real. It's transformative. But transformation takes time.

### The Real Metric

**Bezos:** People focus on the wrong metric. They look at stock prices. They look at bankruptcy filings. What they should look at is: Are people actually using the Internet? Is it growing? Is it becoming more useful?

The answer to all of those is yes. The Internet is not a fad. It's a fundamental shift.

### Long-Term Thinking

**Bezos:** At Amazon, we're building for the long term. We made a decision early on: we would rather have a small piece of a large industry than a large piece of a small industry.

We're not trying to win the quarter. We're trying to win the decade.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Technology Adoption Curves
Bezos emphasized that transformative technologies take decades to reach their full potential.

### 2. The Skeptic's Trap
Short-term failures (dot-com crash) don't invalidate long-term trends.

### 3. Customer Metrics Over Financial Metrics
Focus on actual usage and adoption rather than stock prices.

### 4. Industry Size Philosophy
Better to have small share of massive market than large share of small market.
