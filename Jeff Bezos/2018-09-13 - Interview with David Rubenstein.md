# Jeff Bezos - Interview with David Rubenstein (2018)

**Date:** September 13, 2018  
**Source:** The David Rubenstein Show: Peer to Peer Conversations
**Interviewer:** David Rubenstein  
**Location:** Washington D.C.

---

## About This Interview

Bezos reflected on Amazon's journey, his early decisions, the Washington Post acquisition, and his long-term thinking methodology in this intimate one-on-one conversation.

---
## The Interview

### The Regret Minimization Framework

**David Rubenstein:** You quit a great job on Wall Street to start Amazon. How did you decide?

**Jeff Bezos:** I used the regret minimization framework. I projected myself forward to age 80 and thought about what I would regret at that point. I knew I wouldn't regret trying and failing. I would regret never trying.

**Rubenstein:** You were 30 years old.

**Bezos:** Yes. Still young enough to take risks. Old enough to have some skills.

### The Garage Myth

**Rubenstein:** Is it true you started in a garage?

**Bezos:** It's true, but it's also a bit romanticized. I didn't have a warehouse. My garage was my office. But I wasn't hammering away at code by candlelight or anything.

### On Being the World's Richest Man

**Rubenstein:** You're now the richest person in the world. How does that feel?

**Bezos:** Honestly, I can barely feel it. It's mostly on paper. I feel the same way I did before. What I really care about is Amazon and Blue Origin.

### The Washington Post

**Rubenstein:** Why did you buy the Washington Post?

**Bezos:** It's an important institution. In an era of fake news and declining journalism, someone needed to step up. I have the resources. I believe in the mission.

**Rubenstein:** Do you influence coverage?

**Bezos:** I made a promise: hands-off. The newsroom reports to the editor, not to me.

### On Long-Term Thinking

**Rubenstein:** Why does Amazon sacrifice short-term profits?

**Bezos:** We're not sacrificing them. We're trading them for long-term market leadership. Warren Buffett has a great line: "You can hold a rock concert and that's sustainable, or you can hold a ballet and that's sustainable. But you can't do a rock concert-ballet." We're building a ballet.

---

## Key Insights

### 1. Regret Minimization Framework
Decisions viewed through the lens of "what will I regret at age 80?"

### 2. Long-Term vs. Short-Term
Explicit trade-off: short-term profits for long-term market leadership.

### 3. Reputation and Journalism
Bought Washington Post to preserve an important institution, not to control it.
