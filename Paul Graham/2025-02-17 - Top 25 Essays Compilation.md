# Paul Graham - Top 25 Essays Compilation

**Compiled:** 2025-02-17  
**Source:** paulgraham.com (filtered for most influential)

---

## The Top 25 Essays

### 1. Do Things That Don't Scale (July 2013)
**One-liner:** The counterintuitive advice that startups should begin with unscalable manual work.
**Key insight:** Manually onboard users, personally solve problems, delay automation until you understand what you're automating.
**Impact:** Most-cited PG essay; changed how founders think about early growth.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/ds.html>

---

### 2. Maker's Schedule, Manager's Schedule (July 2009)
**One-liner:** Programmers need long blocks of uninterrupted time; managers schedule in hour increments.
**Key insight:** A single meeting can destroy a maker's entire day. These schedules are incompatible.
**Impact:** Introduced vocabulary for protecting deep work time.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/makersschedule.html>

---

### 3. How to Get Startup Ideas (November 2012)
**One-liner:** Don't try to think of startup ideas. Notice problems you have yourself.
**Key insight:** The best ideas come organically from your own life. Live in the future and build what's missing.
**Impact:** Shifted founder mindset from "ideation" to "problem awareness."
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/startupideas.html>

---

### 4. What You Can't Say (January 2004)
**One-liner:** Moral fashions change. What you can't say today might be obvious tomorrow.
**Key insight:** Keep your identity small. Don't let your beliefs become part of your identity.
**Impact:** Influenced rationalist and contrarian thinking communities.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/say.html>

---

### 5. How to Be Silicon Valley (January 2006)
**One-liner:** What makes Silicon Valley work: nerds and rich people living near each other.
**Key insight:** You need both the talent and the money in the same place. Also need permissive culture.
**Impact:** Shaped understanding of startup ecosystems.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/siliconvalley.html>

---

### 6. How to Start a Startup (March 2005)
**One-liner:** Build something people want. That's it.
**Key insight:** Most founders build first, find customers second. Reverse this.
**Impact:** Foundation of YC philosophy; most-read startup essay.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/start.html>

---

### 7. Hackers and Painters (May 2003)
**One-liner:** Hackers are like painters: makers who learn by doing.
**Key insight:** Programming is a creative act, not just engineering. You learn by making things.
**Impact:** Title essay of his book; shaped hacker culture identity.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/hp.html>

---

### 8. The Age of the Essay (September 2004)
**One-liner:** The essay is the perfect medium for online writing.
**Key insight:** Blogs democratized essay writing. The internet revived the essay as a form.
**Impact:** Legitimized blogging as serious writing.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/essay.html>

---

### 9. How to Think for Yourself (November 2020)
**One-liner:** Smart people can be trapped by conventional wisdom just like anyone else.
**Key insight:** Independent thinking requires: (1) curiosity, (2) resistance to being told what to think, (3) willingness to think about weird things.
**Impact:** Timely essay on intellectual independence.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/think.html>

---

### 10. Superlinear Returns (October 2022)
**One-liner:** In some fields, effort compounds; in others, it doesn't.
**Key insight:** Startups, research, and performance fields have superlinear returns. The best earn 1000x more than average.
**Impact:** Updated thinking on returns in digital/performance domains.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/superlinear.html>

---

### 11. Great Hackers (July 2004)
**One-liner:** Great hackers are 10-100x more productive than average programmers.
**Key insight:** Programming talent follows a power law. Great hackers need: (1) an obsessive interest, (2) the right kind of personality, (3) a good environment.
**Impact:** Shaped hiring and talent recognition in tech.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/gh.html>

---

### 12. How to Make Wealth (May 2004)
**One-liner:** Startups are a way to compress your career into a few intense years.
**Key insight:** To make wealth: (1) start a startup, (2) make something people want, (3) get lots of users, (4) keep the company.
**Impact:** Definitive essay on startup wealth creation.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/wealth.html>

---

### 13. How to Do What You Love (January 2006)
**One-liner:** If you don't know what you love, try different things until you find it.
**Key insight:** "Do what you love" is bad advice if you don't know what you love. You have to discover it.
**Impact:** Nuanced take on career passion.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/love.html>

---

### 14. The Hardest Lessons for Startups to Learn (April 2007)
**One-liner:** Many things founders learn are counterintuitive.
**Key insight:** Hardest lessons: (1) Release early, (2) Talk to users, (3) Don't give up too easily, (4) Startups die from not making something people want.
**Impact:** Required reading for YC founders.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/startuplessons.html>

---

### 15. Why Startup Hubs Work (January 2011)
**One-liner:** Startup hubs work because of conversation.
**Key insight:** The density of startup people means ideas spread fast. You bump into people who can help or challenge you.
**Impact:** Justified the importance of being in startup hubs.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/hubs.html>

---

### 16. How to Disagree (March 2008)
**One-liner:** Disagreement hierarchy from name-calling to refuting the central point.
**Key insight:** Most disagreement is low-quality. The best responses address the opponent's strongest argument.
**Impact:** Became widely-used framework for online discourse.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/disagree.html>

---

### 17. What Is Strategy? (October 2024)
**One-liner:** Strategy is knowing what NOT to do.
**Key insight:** Strategy isn't what you do. It's what you deliberately choose not to do.
**Impact:** Recent essay updating classic business strategy thinking.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/strategy.html>

---

### 18. Schlep Blindness (January 2011)
**One-liner:** Avoidance of tedious tasks blinds you to big opportunities.
**Key insight:** The best startup ideas often involve "schleps" (tedious tasks) that others avoid.
**Impact:** Made founders look for unglamorous opportunities.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/schlep.html>

---

### 19. How to Work Hard (September 2021)
**One-liner:** Working hard is a skill that can be developed.
**Key insight:** Extreme productivity requires: (1) knowing what to work on, (2) caring about it, (3) working hard at it.
**Impact:** Nuanced take on hustle culture.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/hwh.html>

---

### 20. The Island Test (June 2010)
**One-liner:** Would people use your product if they were stranded on an island?
**Key insight:** The best products create their own demand, not just improve existing solutions.
**Impact:** Framework for identifying truly new vs incremental innovations.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/islandtest.html>

---

### 21. Frighteningly Ambitious Startup Ideas (March 2012)
**One-liner:** The best ideas seem crazy at first.
**Key insight:** Ambitious ideas: (1) New payment systems, (2) Replace universities, (3) Internet TV, (4) Next-generation search, (5) Replace email.
**Impact:** Inspired founders to pursue bigger ideas.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/ambitious.html>

---

### 22. How Not to Die (August 2007)
**One-liner:** Startups die when founders lose determination.
**Key insight:** The number one cause of death is not making something people want. The number two is losing the will to continue.
**Impact:** Brutal honesty about startup survival.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/die.html>

---

### 23. The Top of My To-Do List (April 2010)
**One-liner:** Six principles for getting things done.
**Key insight:** (1) Don't ignore your dreams, (2) Don't worry about inexperience, (3) Write for yourself, (4) Don't let your parents hold you back, (5) Don't let your spouse hold you back, (6) Work on hard problems.
**Impact:** Personal productivity and life advice.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/todo.html>

---

### 24. Taste for Makers (February 2002)
**One-liner:** Making good things requires having good taste.
**Key insight:** Taste in design, code, and product is a skill that can be developed.
**Impact:** Early essay on the importance of "taste" in technical work.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/taste.html>

---

### 25. The Submarine (February 2005)
**One-liner:** Most "news" is actually PR planted by companies.
**Key insight:** The media ecosystem is driven by PR firms planting stories. Real news is rare.
**Impact:** Made readers skeptical of tech media.
**Link:** <http://paulgraham.com/submarine.html>

## Themes Across Essays

### 1. Start with the Problem
Almost every essay emphasizes: Don't start with the solution. Start with the problem someone actually has.

### 2. Think for Yourself
Conventional wisdom is often wrong. The best opportunities are where everyone else is wrong.

### 3. Do Unscalable Things First
Growth comes from manual, personal work at the beginning.

### 4. Time Horizon Matters
Most people optimize for weeks or months. Optimize for years or decades.

### 5. Writing is Thinking
PG's output proves that writing is a way to develop and test ideas.

## Key Mental Models from PG

1. **Maker's vs. Manager's Schedule** — Protect deep work blocks
2. **Do Things That Don't Scale** — Manual first, automated later
3. **Schlep Blindness** — Tedium hides opportunity
4. **Superlinear Returns** — Some fields reward excellence disproportionately
5. **Independent Thinking** — Don't let beliefs become identity
6. **Live in the Future** — Build what's missing

## Why Paul Graham Matters

- Founded Y Combinator, the startup accelerator that launched Airbnb, Dropbox, Stripe, Reddit, and thousands more
- Wrote 200+ essays that shaped how a generation thinks about startups
- Created vocabulary for startup concepts ("do things that don't scale", "maker's schedule")
- Combined programming expertise (Lisp) with practical business wisdom
- Maintained quality and independence for 20+ years of writing
